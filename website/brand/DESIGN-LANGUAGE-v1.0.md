## The Page as Identity

Project Garuda's visual identity shall be recognizable through the **page itself** — not primarily through a logo.

Layout, margins, spacing, hierarchy, and restraint constitute the identity.

If the logo were removed, a page should still unmistakably feel like Project Garuda.

Enduring institutional identity lives in behavior, not marks.

---

## Rule 1 — How Pages Breathe

### Principle

Generous space reflects institutional confidence.

Crowded pages suggest urgency Project Garuda does not possess.

### Rules

- Content shall have clear margins on all sides; nothing shall feel edge-to-edge unless intentionally full-bleed for a diagram.
- Vertical rhythm shall be consistent — related elements grouped, unrelated elements separated by visible space.
- One primary reading column shall dominate; secondary columns are permitted only when they serve navigation or reference, not decoration.
- Pages shall feel **room to think** — never **room to sell**.
- Whitespace is active, not empty. It is a design element, not leftover space.

### Never

- Hero sections that consume the entire viewport to impress.
- Stacked promotional blocks competing for attention.
- Density that requires scrolling before the first idea is complete.

---

## Rule 2 — How Hierarchy Feels

### Principle

Hierarchy emerges through spacing and proportion, not ornament.

The reader should know where to begin without being told.

### Rules

- One clear entry point per page — typically the page title or primary proposition.
- Heading levels shall map to institutional importance, not decorative variety.
- Body text shall remain the dominant visual weight; headings guide, they do not dominate.
- Navigation shall be visually subordinate to content — present, calm, never louder than the idea on the page.
- The reading journey shall feel sequential even when navigation offers parallel paths.

### Never

- Multiple elements styled as "most important."
- All-caps headings for emphasis.
- Color or weight used to shout where structure would suffice.

---

## Rule 3 — How Diagrams Behave

### Principle

Every diagram shall increase understanding.

Diagrams inherit the discipline of the Institutional Atlas — explanatory, not decorative.

### Rules

- Diagrams shall explain relationships, sequences, or structures — never mood.
- Line weight, boxes, and arrows shall be restrained and consistent across all public artifacts.
- Labels shall be readable at intended size; abbreviations require glossary context or shall be avoided.
- Diagrams shall align with repository-first authority — they illustrate institutional structure, they do not invent it.
- Complex ideas shall be decomposed into simpler figures rather than presented in a single overwhelming illustration.

### Never

- Stock illustrations substituting for explanation.
- Gradients, glows, or three-dimensional effects without informational purpose.
- Diagrams that require a legend the reader cannot understand in thirty seconds.

---

## Rule 4 — What Makes Something "Garuda"

### Principle

Something feels like Project Garuda when it communicates institutional maturity without requesting applause.

### Garuda feels like

- A university press colophon page.
- A constitutional archive finding aid.
- A research institute working paper cover.
- A carefully typeset preservation volume table of contents.
- A quiet room where serious reading happens.

### Garuda does not feel like

- A product launch landing page.
- A cryptocurrency whitepaper.
- A SaaS pricing page.
- A conference sponsorship brochure.
- An "AI startup" aesthetic.

### Observable qualities

| Quality | Expression |
| --- | --- |
| Calm | No urgency cues, no countdown timers, no aggressive CTAs |
| Clarity | Plain language, obvious structure, visible repository links |
| Permanence | Timeless layout patterns, no trend-driven UI fashions |
| Legitimacy | Authority boundaries visible; website introduces, repository governs |
| Stewardship | Generational framing; institution outlives any page |
| Precision | Exact terminology; no marketing synonyms for constitutional concepts |

---

## Rule 5 — Information Density

### Principle

Project Garuda chooses depth over breadth on any single page.

Density shall serve comprehension, never impress with volume.

### Rules

- One primary purpose per page — introduce, orient, or gateway; not all three at maximum volume.
- Lists shall be short or deliberately structured; walls of bullets are prohibited.
- Tables shall be used for comparison and reference, not for dumping information.
- Long-form content lives in the repository or preservation volumes; public pages gateway to it.
- Progressive disclosure is preferred — curiosity first, depth when the reader chooses it.

### Density guidance

| Page type | Density |
| --- | --- |
| Home | Low — five questions answered in five minutes |
| Philosophy pages | Medium — enough to orient, link to full text |
| Institution gateways | Low — structure and canonical links |
| Library gateways | Low — volume description and repository path |
| Research (future) | Medium to high — when authorized |

### Never

- Everything on one page because the reader might not click again.
- Footnotes competing with body text for visual priority.
- Sidebars that duplicate main content.

## Rule 6 — What Should Never Happen Visually

The following are prohibited in Project Garuda public communication:

### Marketing prohibitions

- Urgency language paired with urgent visual design.
- "Join the revolution" / "The future is here" visual rhetoric.
- Testimonial carousels, star ratings, or social proof widgets.
- Pop-ups, interstitials, or cookie banners that dominate the first visit (when implemented).

### Aesthetic prohibitions

- Neon accents, gradient meshes, or glassmorphism as default style.
- Animated backgrounds or parallax for atmosphere.
- Mascots, cartoon illustrations, or anthropomorphized "AI" imagery.
- Dark-mode-by-default solely to appear "technical."

### Structural prohibitions

- Hiding the repository link or making canonical sources secondary to marketing copy.
- Presenting website content as constitutional or governance authority.
- Visual hierarchy that places brand above institutional substance.

### Behavioral prohibitions

- Infinite scroll where pagination would respect the reader's stopping point.
- Auto-playing media.
- Design that prevents printing or archival capture of a page.

## Relationship to Repository

Every public page shall visibly honor repository-first authority.

Design Language supports this rule:

- Canonical links shall be visually identifiable — not buried in footer gray-on-gray.
- The distinction between **introduction** (website) and **authority** (repository) shall be clear in layout, not only in words.
- Preservation Series volumes linked from the website shall be presented with the same dignity as constitutional references.

---

## Success Criteria

Design Language v1.0 succeeds when:

- A reviewer can evaluate any proposed page against these rules without referring to color or font choices.
- Removing the logo does not remove the institutional character of the page.
- The five-persona navigation test ([Founder Reflection](../../docs/institutional/FOUNDER-REFLECTION-MISSION-001-PHASE-A.md)) still passes when applied to layout and structure alone.
- Typography and color, when later authorized, have clear rules to implement — not ambiguous aesthetic preference.
