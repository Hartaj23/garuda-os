## Selection Recommendation

**Recommended candidate: C-03 — Baseline**

### Rationale for recommendation

At equal numerical score, C-03 Baseline demonstrates the strongest institutional fit because:

- It implements Visual Philosophy Principle III — *Typography Is the Primary Voice* — in mark form.
- It is the quietest candidate at 24px and 16px — maximum subordination to page title and repository callout.
- It references the line on which institutional text rests — the page itself — rather than introducing external symbolism.
- It survives monochrome reproduction without ambiguity at every authorized scale.
- An independent reader would perceive scholarship infrastructure, not brand identity.

### Runner-up for adoption consideration

**C-01 — Threshold** — constitutional gateway metaphor; strongest tie to legitimacy-before-action. Recommended if the Founder prioritizes governance symbolism over typographic acknowledgment.

**C-04 — Open Frame** — strongest tie to invitation and generational stewardship literature. Recommended if the Founder prioritizes openness symbolism.

### Not recommended for adoption

| Candidate | Reason |
| --- | --- |
| C-02 Colophon Frame | Letterform reads toward brand logo at small scale |
| C-05 Archive Imprint | Seal weight competes with subordination requirement |
| C-06 Steward Point | Insufficient distinctiveness at footer scale |
