## Article I — Purpose

This Constitution establishes the constitutional authority governing the **second expansion** of
Project Garuda within the External Capability Expansion epoch, following completion of the Integration
Foundation.

GAR-0018 ratified the first external-capability expansion beyond the Interface Foundation and
authorized the Integration Foundation as the constitutional realization of External Integration.
That mission is complete at release `v0.11.0-alpha`.

This Constitution does **not** amend GAR-0018 or GAR-0017. GAR-0018 and GAR-0017 remain immutable
historical law.

This Constitution establishes, **in order**:

1. Constitutional **principles** governing the second expansion
2. Constitutional **boundaries** of the permitted expansion within the continued epoch
3. **Candidate domain analysis** — auditable rejection of all constitutionally inadmissible domains
4. Constitutional **scope** derived from the remaining admissible domain
5. Authorization of **exactly one** Foundation that satisfies that scope

This Constitution authorizes **architecture and governance only**. It does not authorize
implementation, infrastructure, operational runtime behavior, or external technology integration.

**Core Axiom.** Architecture emerges from constitutional law, not the reverse. The Foundation
authorized by this Constitution is a **consequence** of principles, boundaries, and explicit domain
elimination — not a premise used to justify them.

**Ratification Standard.** A Founder or future architect SHALL be able to read this Constitution
and conclude that **no other second external-capability domain** could satisfy Articles V and VI
following Integration Foundation completion. Article VII exists to make that conclusion auditable.

**Founder Resolution Record.** This draft incorporates Architecture 4 Founder resolutions A1–A7,
including independent constitutional derivation, preservation of Descriptive Before Operational unless
this Constitution demonstrates otherwise through constitutional reasoning, continued External
Capability Expansion epoch designation, exclusion of the GAR-0016 ACP path for new foundation
introduction, and confirmation that GAR-SPRINT-0012 shall be a new foundation sprint.

## Article II — Definitions

**Internal Cognitive Foundations**  
As defined in GAR-0017.

**External Systems**  
As defined in GAR-0017.

**Constitutional Membrane**  
As defined in GAR-0017. Realized exclusively by the Interface Foundation.

**Interface Foundation**  
The completed foundation governing all communication across the Constitutional Membrane, as authorized

**Integration Foundation**  
The completed foundation governing External Integration, as authorized by GAR-0018 and realized by

**External Capability Class**  
A category of external-facing architectural responsibility requiring independent constitutional
authority under GAR-0017 Article VI §4.

**Candidate Domain**  
A proposed External Capability Class evaluated for constitutional admissibility under Articles V and VI
before any Foundation is authorized.

**Admissible Domain**  
A Candidate Domain that survives elimination in Article VII §2.

**Foundation**  
As defined in GAR-0017.

**Constitutional Epoch (External Capability Expansion)**  
The constitutional period governing sequential introduction of external-capability Foundations beyond
the Interface Foundation. This epoch began with GAR-0018 and continues under this Constitution until
constitutional law itself establishes transition to a fundamentally different class of expansion.

**Operational Runtime**  
Runtime behavior that performs execution, operational state transitions, scheduling, invocation of
external behavior, or runtime engine semantics. Operational Runtime is prohibited at this expansion
without further constitutional authority beyond the descriptive scope authorized herein.

**External Runtime Governance**  
The architectural discipline governing how external-facing runtime contexts are described,
classified, bounded, and governed as runtime participants **through** the Integration Foundation and
Constitutional Membrane — without Operational Runtime, transport, orchestration, or provider
implementation.

**Universal Execution Foundation**  
The completed Phase I Internal Cognitive Foundation under `packages.execution` governing cognitive
execution descriptions. This Constitution SHALL NOT conflate Universal Execution Foundation with
External Runtime Governance or Operational Runtime.

**Constitutional Authority**  
As defined in GAR-0017.

Terms specific to the authorized Foundation (including *Runtime Foundation*) are defined in Article IX
upon successful derivation in Article VII.

## Article IV — Constitutional Authority

**§1.** GAR Constitutions establish the highest authority in Project Garuda.

**§2.** This Constitution derives authority from GAR-0018 and GAR-0017 and SHALL NOT contradict,
supersede, or diminish any provision of GAR-0018, GAR-0017, or any completed Internal Cognitive
Foundation.

**§3.** No ADR, sprint specification, mission, implementation, or repository artifact SHALL create,
extend, or infer constitutional authority beyond that granted by this Constitution and its
constitutional predecessors.

**§4. Constitutional Invariant 001 — Authority Flows Downward Only**

As established in GAR-0017.

**§5.** Skipping any stage in the constitutional introduction process SHALL NOT be permitted.

**§6. Relationship to GAR-0018**

GAR-0018 authorized the Integration Foundation as the sole first external-capability expansion
beyond the Interface Foundation and declared that runtime, orchestration, and all other external
capabilities require **independent constitutional authority** not inferable from GAR-0018 alone.
Integration Foundation completion does not exhaust the External Capability Expansion epoch. The
authorized Foundation for the second expansion SHALL be determined only through Article VII — not
assumed in this Article.

**§7. Relationship to GAR-0016**

Introduction of an entirely new Foundation constitutes constitutional expansion, not constitutional
amendment. The GAR-0016 Architecture Change Proposal process is **not** the lawful path for
authorizing the Foundation governed by this Constitution. GAR-0016 remains reserved for genuine
amendments to existing constitutional law.

## Article V — Constitutional Principles Governing the Second Expansion

All boundaries, candidate elimination, scope, and Foundation authorization SHALL derive exclusively
from these principles.

**§1. Membrane Supremacy**  
The Constitutional Membrane SHALL remain exclusive. No Foundation SHALL bypass, duplicate, or weaken
the Interface Foundation as the sole membrane.

**§2. Sequential External Capability Introduction**  
External capabilities beyond the completed Integration Foundation SHALL be introduced through discrete
Foundations, each authorized by independent constitutional authority, one at a time, with full
governance before implementation.

**§3. Descriptive Before Operational**  
The second authorized Foundation SHALL govern **architectural description** — contracts,
classifications, boundaries, relationships, and governance semantics. It SHALL NOT introduce
Operational Runtime, transport, orchestration, scheduling, connectivity operations, or provider
invocation.

This principle is preserved as a constitutional invariant unless this Constitution demonstrates,
through explicit constitutional reasoning in Article VII, that governing principles must evolve.
No such evolution is asserted in Articles I through VI. Article VII tests descriptive admissibility
before any operational authority could exist.

**§4. Technology Neutrality Continuation**  
The second authorized Foundation SHALL remain independent of all external technologies.

**§5. Additive Evolution**  
This expansion SHALL extend Project Garuda additively without redefining GAR-0018, GAR-0017, the
Interface Foundation, the Integration Foundation, or any Internal Cognitive Foundation except
through GAR-0016.

**§6. Cognitive Separation Continuation**  
The second authorized Foundation SHALL NOT perform cognition.

**§7. Variability Containment Extension**  
External variability SHALL continue to terminate at constitutionally authorized external-capability
Foundations and SHALL NOT propagate into Internal Cognitive Foundations.

**§8. Governance Before Repository**  
Every new capability SHALL enter through governance before the repository.

**§9. Stack Traversal**  
The second authorized Foundation SHALL operate **through** the Interface Foundation and Integration
Foundation. It SHALL NOT bypass, duplicate, or weaken either completed foundation.

**§10. First Gap After Integration**  
The second authorized Foundation SHALL address the **first External Capability Class** that:

1. Is not yet constitutionally governed after Integration Foundation completion,
2. Is logically subsequent to Integration Foundation establishment,
3. Does not require capabilities prohibited by Article VI §2,
4. Satisfies Descriptive Before Operational (§3),
5. Is analyzable before Orchestration under the binding precedent that Orchestration presupposes
   Runtime — an ordering constraint, not an outcome guarantee.

This principle constrains the expansion to the earliest lawful external capability following
Integration — not an arbitrary product preference.

**§11. Distinction from Universal Execution Foundation**  
External Runtime Governance SHALL remain constitutionally distinct from the Phase I Universal
Execution Foundation. Phase I execution descriptions govern internal cognitive execution semantics.
External Runtime Governance governs external-facing runtime context description in the
external-capability stack. Neither SHALL be conflated in derivation, scope, or dependency law.

## Article VI — Constitutional Boundaries

**§1. Immutable Predecessors**

The following SHALL NOT be modified:

- GAR-0017 and all provisions therein
- GAR-0018 and all provisions therein
- All Internal Cognitive Foundations (Phase I)
- The Interface Foundation and Constitutional Membrane semantics
- The Integration Foundation and External Integration semantics
- Platform Core object identity, validation, and serialization law

**§2. Prohibited Capabilities Without Further Constitutional Authority**

This expansion SHALL NOT introduce:

- Network protocols, APIs, HTTP, REST, gRPC, WebSockets
- Databases, persistence, message queues
- Authentication, authorization, identity systems
- LLM providers, MCP, or any specific external technology
- Runtime execution engines
- Orchestration engines
- Scheduling systems
- Deployment, containers, or cloud platforms
- Transport layers or provider implementations
- Operational integration execution or operational runtime invocation

These prohibitions inherit from GAR-0017 Article VI §2 and GAR-0018 Article VI §2.

**§3. Single Foundation Authorization**

This Constitution authorizes **exactly one** new Foundation.

**§4. Membrane and Stack Exclusivity Preservation**

All external-capability Foundations SHALL operate **through** the Interface Foundation and lawful
predecessors in the external-capability stack, not around them.

**§5. Epoch Designation**

This Constitution continues the **External Capability Expansion** epoch established by GAR-0018. It
does not designate Phase III and does not introduce a new epoch merely because a second foundation is
being authorized.

**§6. Rejected Architectural Alternatives Remain in Force**

Architectural alternatives rejected by ADR-0011 and ADR-0012 — including direct provider integration
into cognitive foundations, multiple interface boundaries, provider-specific layers, operational
integration execution, and technology-specific external-capability architecture — remain
constitutionally rejected.

**§7. Sprint Scope Boundary**

GAR-SPRINT-0012, when authorized, SHALL implement the Foundation authorized by this Constitution
only. It is not an Integration enhancement sprint, governance maintenance sprint, operational
connectivity sprint, or refactoring sprint.

## Article VII — Candidate Domain Analysis and Constitutional Derivation

This Article is the auditable derivation record. No Foundation is authorized until §3 is satisfied.

**Candidate Completeness.** Article VII evaluates every constitutionally plausible second
external-capability domain derivable from GAR-0018, GAR-0017, ADR-0011, ADR-0012, and the
constitutional principles established in this document. The Candidate Domains in §1 comprise:

- Every remaining External Capability Class explicitly named in GAR-0017 Article VI §4 not yet
  constitutionally governed (runtime, orchestration),
- Integration re-extension or Integration operationalization patterns,
- Every logically adjacent external-capability pattern that could satisfy Article V §10 without
  violating Article VI §2,
- Every alternative that would duplicate, bypass, or weaken the Constitutional Membrane, Interface
  Foundation, Integration Foundation, or reintroduce alternatives rejected by ADR-0011 or ADR-0012.

No additional constitutionally plausible second domain has been identified at the time of drafting. A
future reviewer challenging exhaustiveness must identify an additional Candidate Domain and
demonstrate that it satisfies Articles V and VI simultaneously — otherwise the elimination record in
§2 stands.

**Interpretive Note on GAR-0018 Runtime Rejection.** GAR-0018 Article VII rejected Candidate A
(Runtime) at the **first** external-capability expansion because Integration was the first lawful
gap after membrane establishment and because **Operational Runtime** interpretation violates
Descriptive Before Operational at that epoch. That rejection does **not** permanently eliminate
constitutional analysis of **External Runtime Governance** as a distinct descriptive External
Capability Class at the post-Integration epoch. GAR-0019 re-evaluates Runtime under Article V §3
and §10.

### §2. Domain Elimination

```
Candidate A — Operational Runtime
        ↓ REJECTED
Operational Runtime performs execution, operational state transitions, scheduling, or invocation of
external behavior.
Article VI §2 prohibits runtime execution engines and scheduling systems without further
constitutional authority beyond descriptive scope.
Principle §3 (Descriptive Before Operational) prohibits Operational Runtime at this expansion.
Article V §10 — Operational Runtime fails the First Gap After Integration test as a descriptive
foundation because it is operational by definition.
GAR-0019 does not evolve Principle §3 to authorize Operational Runtime. Constitutional evolution is
not demonstrated as necessary because a descriptive admissible domain survives in Candidate B.

Candidate C — Orchestration
        ↓ REJECTED (ordering: analyzed after Runtime candidates)
Orchestration necessarily coordinates operational execution, process sequencing, or runtime
dependencies across external or internal participants.
Article VI §2 prohibits orchestration engines without further constitutional authority.
Binding precedent (GAR-0018 Article VII; Architecture 4 Founder Resolution A4): Orchestration
presupposes Runtime — an ordering constraint requiring Runtime analysis first, not an outcome
guarantee that Orchestration follows Runtime authorization.
Even after Integration completion, Orchestration remains operational, not descriptive — violating
Principle §3.
Article V §10 — Orchestration fails Descriptive Before Operational at this expansion epoch.

Candidate D — Integration Re-extension / Operationalization
        ↓ REJECTED
The Integration Foundation is complete, published, and immutable.
Operational integration execution is explicitly excluded from GAR-0018 and GAR-SPRINT-0011 scope.
Extending Integration into operational behavior would violate Descriptive Before Operational and
Article VI §2 rather than introduce a distinct External Capability Class.
Architecture 4 Founder Resolution A7 — GAR-SPRINT-0012 is a new foundation sprint, not an
Integration enhancement sprint.

Candidate E — Transport / Connectivity
        ↓ REJECTED
Transport necessarily performs connectivity, message delivery, protocol handling, or network
communication.
Article VI §2 prohibits transport layers and network protocols without further constitutional
authority.
Transport is operational, not descriptive — violating Principle §3.
Principle §1 and §9 — Transport at a new layer would duplicate or bypass membrane and integration
responsibilities.

Candidate F — Secondary Interface / Membrane
        ↓ REJECTED
Principle §1 (Membrane Supremacy) — exactly one membrane exists.
GAR-0017 Art V §1 and ADR-0011 Principle 1 — exclusive boundary.
Article VI §6 — ADR-0011 rejected multiple interface boundaries.

Candidate G — Provider Abstraction
        ↓ REJECTED
Provider abstraction necessarily embeds provider-specific architectural commitments.
Principle §4 (Technology Neutrality) — provider-specific architecture is prohibited.
Article VI §6 — ADR-0011 and ADR-0012 rejected provider-specific patterns.
Article VI §2 — prohibits specific external technologies and provider implementations.

Candidate H — Authentication / Identity
        ↓ REJECTED
Authentication and identity necessarily govern credential validation, authorization decisions, or
identity lifecycle operations.
Article VI §2 explicitly prohibits authentication, authorization, and identity systems without
further constitutional authority.
This domain does not satisfy Article V §10 as the first gap after Integration completion.

Candidate I — Persistence
        ↓ REJECTED
Persistence necessarily introduces databases, durable storage, or message queues.
Article VI §2 explicitly prohibits databases, persistence, and message queues without further

Candidate J — Deployment / Infrastructure
        ↓ REJECTED
Deployment necessarily manages containers, cloud platforms, or infrastructure lifecycle operations.
Article VI §2 explicitly prohibits deployment, containers, and cloud platforms without further
constitutional authority.
Deployment is operational — violating Principle §3.

Candidate K — Outbound Operational Representation
        ↓ REJECTED
Outbound operational representation necessarily performs provider invocation, delivery semantics, or
transport-adjacent operational behavior.
Principle §3 — such behavior is operational and prohibited at this epoch.
Canonical communication semantics across the membrane remain governed by the completed Interface
Foundation. Integration participant semantics remain governed by the completed Integration
Foundation. A separate outbound operational domain would duplicate stack obligations or introduce
prohibited operational behavior.

Candidate L — Direct Cognitive–External Bridge
        ↓ REJECTED
Principle §1 — violates Membrane Supremacy.
GAR-0017 Art V §2–§3 — violates Cognitive Purity and External Isolation.
ADR-0011 rejected direct provider integration into cognitive foundations.
This candidate is constitutionally impermissible under all predecessor law.

Candidate M — Universal Execution Foundation Extension
        ↓ REJECTED
Principle §11 — External Runtime Governance SHALL NOT be conflated with Phase I Universal
Execution Foundation.
Reclassifying Phase I execution semantics as external runtime would modify completed Phase I law

under GAR-0017 Article VI §4.
The Universal Execution Foundation is complete and immutable.

Candidate B — External Runtime Governance (descriptive)
        ↓ ADMISSIBLE
GAR-0017 Art VI §4 explicitly names runtime as requiring independent constitutional authority.
External Runtime Governance, as architectural description of how external-facing runtime contexts
are classified, bounded, contractually governed, and related as runtime participants **through**
Integration and the membrane, is descriptive rather than operational — satisfying Principle §3 when

Principle §1 and §9 — operates through Interface and Integration Foundations; does not duplicate
membrane or integration participant semantics.
Article VI §2 — does not require prohibited Operational Runtime engines, transport, orchestration,
persistence, auth, deployment, or provider implementation at this epoch.
Principle §11 — remains distinct from Universal Execution Foundation.
Article V §10 — addresses the first External Capability Class logically subsequent to Integration
Foundation completion: how external-facing runtime contexts are architecturally governed as
descriptive runtime participants without Operational Runtime.
Article VI §6 — does not reintroduce rejected ADR-0011 or ADR-0012 alternatives.
Descriptive Before Operational is preserved; Operational Runtime interpretation is rejected in
Candidate A without requiring constitutional evolution of Principle §3.
```

## Article VIII — Constitutional Scope

Scope is derived **only** from the admissible External Runtime Governance domain established in
Article VII §3.

**§1. Permitted Scope**

This expansion authorizes architectural governance of:

- External-facing runtime context descriptions traversing the Integration Foundation and
  Constitutional Membrane
- Runtime participant classification and boundary semantics — **descriptive only**
- Runtime contract governance subordinate to integration contracts and canonical interface contracts
- Runtime lifecycle and boundary metadata — **descriptive only**
- Runtime registry or catalog semantics — **descriptive only**
- Certification and dependency law for the authorized Foundation

**§2. Scope Exclusions**

- Operational Runtime execution engines
- Runtime invocation, scheduling, or operational state transition engines
- Message delivery, connectivity, or transport
- Orchestration of external or internal processes
- Outbound provider invocation
- Persistence beyond process-local descriptive catalogs
- Any capability in Article VI §2
- Any conflation with Universal Execution Foundation cognitive semantics

## Article IX — Foundation Authorization

**§1. Definitions (upon derivation)**

**External Runtime Governance**  
The architectural discipline governing how external-facing runtime contexts are described,
classified, bounded, and governed as runtime participants **through** the Integration Foundation
and Constitutional Membrane — without Operational Runtime, transport, orchestration, or provider
implementation.

**Runtime Foundation**  
The foundational layer constitutionally responsible for governing External Runtime Governance
within Article VIII scope.

**§2. Authorization**

Applying Articles V through VIII, this Constitution authorizes exactly one Foundation:

**The Runtime Foundation**

**§3. Constitutional Responsibility**

The Runtime Foundation SHALL govern External Runtime Governance as defined in §1.

**§4. Constitutional Obligations**

The Runtime Foundation SHALL preserve all principles in Article V and boundaries in Article VI.

**§5. Explicit Non-Responsibilities**

The Runtime Foundation is **not**: a runtime execution engine; an orchestration engine; a transport
layer; a provider implementation; a substitute membrane; an integration participant classifier; a
scheduling system; a cognitive foundation; or the Universal Execution Foundation.

**§6. Derivation Conclusion**

The Runtime Foundation is authorized because External Runtime Governance is the only admissible
external-capability domain established in Article VII §3. Foundation authorization is not asserted in
Article VII; it follows here as a constitutional consequence of domain derivation.

## Article X — Dependency Law

**§1.** Dependencies flow toward Internal Cognitive Foundations and lawfully ordered
external-capability Foundations.

**§2.** Internal Cognitive Foundations SHALL NOT depend upon External Systems, the Interface
Foundation, the Integration Foundation, or the Runtime Foundation.

**§3.** The Interface Foundation and Integration Foundation SHALL remain immutable. The Runtime
Foundation MAY depend upon the Integration Foundation, Interface Foundation, and Internal Cognitive
Foundations as required by lawful stack traversal.

**§4.** The Runtime Foundation SHALL depend upon the Integration Foundation and Interface Foundation
and SHALL NOT create dependencies from Internal Cognitive Foundations toward External Systems.

**§5.** Circular dependencies are prohibited.

**§6.** Completed Foundations remain immutable unless GAR-0016 authorizes otherwise.

**§7. External-Capability Stack Order**

At authorization of this Constitution, the lawful external-capability stack order is:

```
Interface Foundation (Constitutional Membrane)
        ↓
Integration Foundation (External Integration)
        ↓
Runtime Foundation (External Runtime Governance)
```

Future external-capability Foundations require independent constitutional authority and SHALL
respect this ordering unless explicitly re-derived and ratified.
