## Article I — Purpose

This Constitution establishes the constitutional authority governing the **first expansion** of
Project Garuda beyond the completed Interface Foundation.

GAR-0017 ratified the Constitutional Membrane and authorized the Interface Foundation as the
exclusive boundary for communication between External Systems and Internal Cognitive Foundations.
That mission is complete.

This Constitution does **not** amend GAR-0017. GAR-0017 remains immutable historical law.

This Constitution establishes, **in order**:

1. Constitutional **principles** governing the next expansion
2. Constitutional **boundaries** of the permitted expansion epoch
3. **Candidate domain analysis** — auditable rejection of all constitutionally inadmissible domains
4. Constitutional **scope** derived from the remaining admissible domain
5. Authorization of **exactly one** Foundation that satisfies that scope

This Constitution authorizes **architecture and governance only**. It does not authorize
implementation, infrastructure, runtime behavior, or external technology integration.

**Core Axiom.** Architecture emerges from constitutional law, not the reverse. The Foundation
authorized by this Constitution is a **consequence** of principles, boundaries, and explicit domain
elimination — not a premise used to justify them.

**Ratification Standard.** A Founder or future architect SHALL be able to read this Constitution
and conclude that **no other first external-capability domain** could satisfy Articles V and VI.
Article VII exists to make that conclusion auditable.

## Article IV — Constitutional Authority

**§1.** GAR Constitutions establish the highest authority in Project Garuda.

**§2.** This Constitution derives authority from GAR-0017 and SHALL NOT contradict, supersede, or
diminish any provision of GAR-0017 or any completed Internal Cognitive Foundation.

**§3.** No ADR, sprint specification, mission, implementation, or repository artifact SHALL create,
extend, or infer constitutional authority beyond that granted by this Constitution and its
constitutional predecessors.

**§4. Constitutional Invariant 001 — Authority Flows Downward Only**

As established in GAR-0017.

**§5.** Skipping any stage in the constitutional introduction process SHALL NOT be permitted.

**§6. Relationship to GAR-0017**

GAR-0017 authorized the Interface Foundation as the sole Phase II Foundation under that Constitution
and declared that integration, runtime, orchestration, and all other external capabilities require
**independent constitutional authority**. This Constitution is the first such independent authority
following Interface Foundation completion. The authorized Foundation SHALL be determined only through
Article VII — not assumed in this Article.

## Article V — Constitutional Principles Governing the Next Expansion

All boundaries, candidate elimination, scope, and Foundation authorization SHALL derive exclusively
from these principles.

**§1. Membrane Supremacy**  
The Constitutional Membrane SHALL remain exclusive. No Foundation SHALL bypass, duplicate, or weaken
the Interface Foundation as the sole membrane.

**§2. Sequential External Capability Introduction**  
External capabilities beyond the Constitutional Membrane SHALL be introduced through discrete
Foundations, each authorized by independent constitutional authority, one at a time, with full
governance before implementation.

**§3. Descriptive Before Operational**  
The next authorized Foundation SHALL govern **architectural description** — contracts,
classifications, relationships, and governance semantics. It SHALL NOT introduce runtime execution,
transport, orchestration, scheduling, connectivity operations, or provider invocation.

**§4. Technology Neutrality Continuation**  
The next authorized Foundation SHALL remain independent of all external technologies.

**§5. Additive Evolution**  
This expansion SHALL extend Project Garuda additively without redefining GAR-0017, the Interface
Foundation, or any Internal Cognitive Foundation except through GAR-0016.

**§6. Cognitive Separation Continuation**  
The next authorized Foundation SHALL NOT perform cognition.

**§7. Variability Containment Extension**  
External variability SHALL continue to terminate at constitutionally authorized external-capability
Foundations and SHALL NOT propagate into Internal Cognitive Foundations.

**§8. Governance Before Repository**  
Every new capability SHALL enter through governance before the repository.

**§9. First Gap After Membrane**  
The next authorized Foundation SHALL address the **first External Capability Class** that:

1. Is not yet constitutionally governed after Interface Foundation completion,
2. Is logically subsequent to membrane establishment,
3. Does not require capabilities prohibited by Article VI §2,
4. Satisfies Descriptive Before Operational (§3).

This principle constrains the expansion to the earliest lawful external capability — not an arbitrary
product preference.

## Article VI — Constitutional Boundaries

**§1. Immutable Predecessors**

The following SHALL NOT be modified:

- GAR-0017 and all provisions therein
- All Internal Cognitive Foundations (Phase I)
- The Interface Foundation and Constitutional Membrane semantics
- Platform Core object identity, validation, and serialization law

**§2. Prohibited Capabilities Without Further Constitutional Authority**

This expansion epoch SHALL NOT introduce:

- Network protocols, APIs, HTTP, REST, gRPC, WebSockets
- Databases, persistence, message queues
- Authentication, authorization, identity systems
- LLM providers, MCP, or any specific external technology
- Runtime execution engines
- Orchestration engines
- Scheduling systems
- Deployment, containers, or cloud platforms
- Transport layers or provider implementations

These prohibitions inherit from GAR-0017 Article VI §2.

**§3. Single Foundation Authorization**

This Constitution authorizes **exactly one** new Foundation.

**§4. Membrane Exclusivity Preservation**

All external-capability Foundations SHALL operate **through** the Interface Foundation, not around it.

**§5. Phase Designation**

This Constitution establishes an **External Capability Expansion** epoch. It does not designate
Phase III.

**§6. ADR-0011 Rejected Alternatives Remain in Force**

Architectural alternatives rejected by ADR-0011 — including direct provider integration into
cognitive foundations, multiple interface boundaries, provider-specific interface layers, and
technology-specific interface architecture — remain constitutionally rejected.

## Article VII — Candidate Domain Analysis and Constitutional Derivation

This Article is the auditable derivation record. No Foundation is authorized until §3 is satisfied.

**Candidate Completeness.** Article VII evaluates every constitutionally plausible first
external-capability domain derivable from GAR-0017, ADR-0011, and the constitutional principles
established in this document. The eleven Candidate Domains in §1 comprise:

- Every External Capability Class explicitly named in GAR-0017 Article VI §4 (integration, runtime,
  orchestration),
- Every logically adjacent external-capability pattern that could satisfy Article V §9 without
  violating Article VI §2,
- Every alternative that would duplicate, bypass, or weaken the Constitutional Membrane or reintroduce
  alternatives rejected by ADR-0011.

No additional constitutionally plausible first domain has been identified. A future reviewer challenging
exhaustiveness must identify a twelfth Candidate Domain and demonstrate that it satisfies Articles V
and VI simultaneously — otherwise the elimination record in §2 stands.

### §1. Candidate Domains Evaluated

The following Candidate Domains were evaluated as potential first external-capability domains beyond
the Interface Foundation. Each is assessed against Articles V and VI and GAR-0017 Article VI §4.

### §2. Domain Elimination

```
Candidate A — Runtime
        ↓ REJECTED
Runtime necessarily performs execution, operational state transitions, scheduling, or invocation of
external behavior.
Article VI §2 prohibits runtime execution engines and scheduling systems without further constitutional
authority.
Principle §3 (Descriptive Before Operational) prohibits operational behavior at this expansion epoch.
Runtime therefore necessarily violates Principle §3 and Article VI §2.
Article V §9 — Runtime fails the First Gap After Membrane test.

Candidate B — Orchestration
        ↓ REJECTED
Orchestration necessarily coordinates operational execution, process sequencing, or runtime
dependencies across external or internal participants.
Article VI §2 prohibits orchestration engines without further constitutional authority.
Orchestration presupposes runtime execution and operational coordination — both prohibited at this
epoch.
Principle §3 — Orchestration is operational, not descriptive.
Article V §9 — Orchestration fails Descriptive Before Operational.

Candidate D — Transport / Connectivity
        ↓ REJECTED
Transport necessarily performs connectivity, message delivery, protocol handling, or network
communication.
Article VI §2 prohibits transport layers and network protocols without further constitutional authority.
Transport is operational, not descriptive — violating Principle §3.
Principle §1 (Membrane Supremacy) — a Transport domain at a new layer would duplicate or bypass
membrane communication responsibilities assigned to the Interface Foundation.

Candidate E — Secondary Interface / Membrane
        ↓ REJECTED
Principle §1 (Membrane Supremacy) — exactly one membrane exists.
GAR-0017 Art V §1 and ADR-0011 Principle 1 — exclusive boundary.
Article VI §6 — ADR-0011 rejected multiple interface boundaries.

Candidate F — Provider Abstraction
        ↓ REJECTED
Provider abstraction necessarily embeds provider-specific architectural commitments.
Principle §4 (Technology Neutrality) — provider-specific architecture is prohibited.
Article VI §6 — ADR-0011 rejected provider-specific interface layers.
Article VI §2 — prohibits specific external technologies and provider implementations.

Candidate G — Authentication / Identity
        ↓ REJECTED
Authentication and identity necessarily govern credential validation, authorization decisions, or
identity lifecycle operations.
Article VI §2 explicitly prohibits authentication, authorization, and identity systems without
further constitutional authority.
This domain does not satisfy Article V §9 as the first gap after membrane establishment.

Candidate H — Persistence
        ↓ REJECTED
Persistence necessarily introduces databases, durable storage, or message queues.
Article VI §2 explicitly prohibits databases, persistence, and message queues without further
constitutional authority.
Persistence is not the first logical gap after membrane establishment under Article V §9.

Candidate I — Deployment / Infrastructure
        ↓ REJECTED
Deployment necessarily manages containers, cloud platforms, or infrastructure lifecycle operations.
Article VI §2 explicitly prohibits deployment, containers, and cloud platforms without further
constitutional authority.
Deployment is operational — violating Principle §3.
Infrastructure management is not the first descriptive external gap under Article V §9.

Candidate J — Outbound Operational Representation
        ↓ REJECTED
Outbound operational representation necessarily performs provider invocation, delivery semantics, or
transport-adjacent operational behavior.
Principle §3 — such behavior is operational and prohibited at this epoch.
Canonical communication semantics across the membrane are governed by the completed Interface
Foundation. A separate outbound operational domain would duplicate membrane obligations or introduce
prohibited operational behavior.
Any future outbound descriptive contract extension beyond current Interface scope requires Interface
amendment through GAR-0016 — not a new first external-capability domain at this epoch.

Candidate K — Direct Cognitive–External Bridge
        ↓ REJECTED
Principle §1 — violates Membrane Supremacy.
GAR-0017 Art V §2–§3 — violates Cognitive Purity and External Isolation.
ADR-0011 rejected direct provider integration into cognitive foundations.
This candidate is constitutionally impermissible under all predecessor law.

Candidate C — Integration
        ↓ ADMISSIBLE
GAR-0017 Art VI §4 explicitly names integration as requiring independent constitutional authority.
Integration, as architectural description of how External Systems participate as governed integration
participants **through** the membrane, is descriptive rather than operational — satisfying Principle §3.
Principle §1 — operates through the Interface Foundation; does not duplicate the membrane.
Article VI §2 — does not require prohibited runtime, transport, orchestration, persistence, auth,
deployment, or provider implementation at this epoch.
Article V §9 — addresses the first External Capability Class logically subsequent to membrane
establishment: how External Systems are architecturally integrated as participants without operational
execution.
Article VI §6 — does not reintroduce rejected ADR-0011 alternatives.
```

### §3. Remaining Admissible Domain

After elimination, **exactly one** Candidate Domain remains admissible:

**Integration** — the External Capability Class governing architectural description of External
Systems as integration participants through the Constitutional Membrane.

No other Candidate Domain in §1 survives Articles V and VI simultaneously.

### §4. Uniqueness Proof

**Question:** Could a reasonable future architect, applying only Articles V and VI and GAR-0017,
select a different first external-capability domain?

**Answer:** No.

| Alternative basis | Why it fails |
| --- | --- |
| Select Runtime first | Prohibited by Principle §3 and Article VI §2 — see §2 Candidate A |
| Select Orchestration first | Prohibited by Principle §3 and Article VI §2 — see §2 Candidate B |
| Select a domain outside GAR-0017 §4 naming | Must still satisfy Article V §9 first-gap test; all evaluated alternatives eliminated in §2 |
| Decline expansion at this epoch | Permissible at governance level — CEG authorizes drafting, not mandatory expansion. However, **if** constitutional expansion proceeds, no domain other than Integration satisfies Articles V and VI |
| Select multiple domains | Violated by Article VI §3 — exactly one domain admissible; exactly one Foundation authorized in Article IX |
| Extend Interface instead of new domain | Interface Foundation is complete and immutable; integration is a distinct External Capability Class per GAR-0017 §4, not membrane communication semantics |

Therefore **Integration** is the only constitutionally admissible external-capability domain.
Foundation authorization follows in Article IX.

### §5. Reproducibility Statement

An independent architect applying:

1. GAR-0017 (immutable),
2. Articles V and VI of this Constitution,
3. The elimination record in §2,

SHALL reach the identical admissible domain: **Integration**.

Disagreement would require either misapplication of a prohibition in Article VI §2 or acceptance of
a Candidate Domain explicitly rejected in §2.

## Article VIII — Constitutional Scope

Scope is derived **only** from the admissible Integration domain established in Article VII §3.

**§1. Permitted Scope**

This expansion epoch authorizes architectural governance of:

- External system integration descriptions traversing the Constitutional Membrane
- Integration participant classification and relationship semantics
- Integration contract governance subordinate to canonical interface contracts
- Integration lifecycle and boundary semantics — **descriptive only**
- Integration registry or catalog semantics — **descriptive only**
- Certification and dependency law for the authorized Foundation

**§2. Scope Exclusions**

- Operational integration execution
- Message delivery, connectivity, or transport
- Orchestration of external or internal processes
- Outbound provider invocation
- Persistence beyond process-local descriptive catalogs
- Any capability in Article VI §2

## Article IX — Foundation Authorization

**§1. Definitions (upon derivation)**

**External Integration**  
The architectural discipline governing how External Systems are described, classified, related, and
governed as integration participants **through** the Constitutional Membrane — without runtime
execution, transport, orchestration, or provider implementation.

**Integration Foundation**  
The foundational layer constitutionally responsible for governing External Integration within
Article VIII scope.

**§2. Authorization**

Applying Articles V through VIII, this Constitution authorizes exactly one Foundation:

**The Integration Foundation**

**§3. Constitutional Responsibility**

The Integration Foundation SHALL govern External Integration as defined in §1.

**§4. Constitutional Obligations**

The Integration Foundation SHALL preserve all principles in Article V and boundaries in Article VI.

**§5. Explicit Non-Responsibilities**

The Integration Foundation is **not**: a transport layer; a runtime or orchestration engine; a
provider implementation; a substitute membrane; an execution or scheduling system; a cognitive
foundation.

**§6. Derivation Conclusion**

The Integration Foundation is authorized because Integration is the only admissible
external-capability domain established in Article VII §3. Foundation authorization is not asserted
in Article VII; it follows here as a constitutional consequence of domain derivation.

## Article X — Dependency Law

**§1.** Dependencies flow toward Internal Cognitive Foundations and lawfully ordered
external-capability Foundations.

**§2.** Internal Cognitive Foundations SHALL NOT depend upon External Systems, the Interface
Foundation, or the Integration Foundation.

**§3.** The Interface Foundation SHALL remain immutable. The Integration Foundation MAY depend upon
the Interface Foundation and Internal Cognitive Foundations.

**§4.** The Integration Foundation SHALL depend upon the Interface Foundation and SHALL NOT create
dependencies from Internal Cognitive Foundations toward External Systems.

**§5.** Circular dependencies are prohibited.

**§6.** Completed Foundations remain immutable unless GAR-0016 authorizes otherwise.
