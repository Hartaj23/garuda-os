# The Steward's Charge

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Document Type | Institutional Literature |
| Authority | Informational (Non-Constitutional) |
| Edition | First Edition |
| Version | 1.0 |

This charge creates no constitutional, architectural, sprint, or engineering authority.

---

This library does not ask you to preserve every conclusion.

It asks you to preserve every standard by which those conclusions were reached.

You inherit an institution that values explicit authority over assumption, evidence over convenience, and stewardship over momentum.

Those values are now entrusted to your care.

Read before you revise.

Understand before you extend.

Preserve before you replace.

When constitutional questions arise, let them arise honestly.

When governance is required, conduct it transparently.

When authority is granted, exercise it carefully.

When engineering begins, ensure it inherits legitimate authority.

When preservation becomes your responsibility, remember that future generations cannot inherit reasoning that was never recorded.

There will come a day when you know more than those who prepared this edition.

That is not a reason to disregard their work.

It is a reason to understand it deeply before deciding where it should evolve.

Respect does not require agreement.

Stewardship requires understanding.

If the institution one day asks questions that these volumes could not have imagined, answer them with the same discipline that produced the First Constitutional Epoch.

Do not imitate the past.

Learn from it.

Do not preserve these books because they are old.

Preserve them because they explain how the institution learned to govern itself.

And when your own stewardship draws to a close, leave behind an inheritance that is clearer than the one you received.

If you do that, the institution will continue to mature long after every name recorded in these pages has been forgotten.

The true measure of stewardship is not that future generations remember their predecessors.

It is that they inherit an institution worthy of remembering.

---

End of The Steward's Charge
