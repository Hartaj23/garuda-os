# Editor's Introduction

This introduction creates no constitutional, architectural, sprint, or engineering authority.

---

Every enduring institution eventually reaches a point where its foundational texts deserve to be read directly.

The previous volumes of the Preservation Series prepared the reader for that moment.

Book One narrated the institution's journey.
The Institutional Atlas illustrated its structure.
The Companion & Glossary explained its language.
The Institutional Timeline preserved its chronology.

This volume now presents the institution's own constitutional voice.

Unlike the preceding volumes, **Selected Constitutional Texts** contains as little editorial interpretation as possible.

Its purpose is not to explain the constitutional corpus.
Its purpose is to make the constitutional corpus approachable without altering its meaning.

Each selected artifact is presented because it represents a significant moment in the constitutional evolution of Project Garuda.

Editorial introductions provide only the historical context necessary to orient the reader.
The constitutional text itself remains unchanged.

Nothing in this volume creates constitutional authority.
Nothing amends existing constitutional artifacts.
Nothing replaces the repository as the institution's canonical record.

The repository remains the source of authority.
This volume exists only to help future stewards engage with that authority more thoughtfully.

The selections presented here are not intended to be exhaustive.
They are intended to illuminate the constitutional journey of the First Constitutional Epoch through the documents that most clearly shaped its development.

Readers are encouraged to consult the original repository artifacts whenever deeper study is required.

The constitutional texts belong to the institution.
This volume simply gathers them into a form that can be read continuously, studied carefully, and preserved across generations.

If the earlier volumes taught the reader how to approach Project Garuda, this volume invites the reader to encounter the institution in its own words.

That is its purpose.

---

End of Editor's Introduction
