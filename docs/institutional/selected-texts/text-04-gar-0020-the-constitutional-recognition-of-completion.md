## Editorial Introduction

GAR-0020 is one of the most distinctive constitutional artifacts within the history of Project Garuda.

Its importance does not arise from introducing another constitutional foundation.

Its importance arises from concluding, after disciplined constitutional evaluation, that no further descriptive external-capability foundation was constitutionally justified under the governing principles of the First Constitutional Epoch.

Viewed historically, GAR-0020 represents the institution's willingness to distinguish possibility from legitimacy.

The institution acknowledged that additional concepts could certainly be imagined.

It also concluded that imagination alone does not constitute constitutional authority.

In doing so, GAR-0020 transformed constitutional restraint into a positive institutional achievement.

The constitutional text that follows is reproduced exactly as ratified.

No editorial interpretation alters its wording, structure, or constitutional authority.

## Article I — Purpose

This Constitution establishes the constitutional authority governing the **third derivation** within
the External Capability Expansion epoch, following completion of the Runtime Foundation at release
`v0.12.0-alpha`.

GAR-0019 ratified the second external-capability expansion and authorized the Runtime Foundation as
the constitutional realization of External Runtime Governance. That mission is complete.

This Constitution does **not** amend GAR-0019, GAR-0018, or GAR-0017. GAR-0019, GAR-0018, and
GAR-0017 remain immutable historical law.

This Constitution establishes, **in order**:

1. Constitutional **principles** governing the third derivation
2. Constitutional **boundaries** of the permitted analysis within the continued epoch
3. **Candidate domain analysis** — auditable rejection of all constitutionally inadmissible domains
4. Constitutional **conclusion** derived from elimination results
5. Either authorization of **exactly one** Foundation **or** explicit **epoch pause** declaration

This Constitution authorizes **constitutional and governance analysis only**. It does not authorize
implementation, infrastructure, operational orchestration behavior, ADR drafting, sprint
specification, or engineering.

**Core Axiom.** Architecture emerges from constitutional law, not the reverse. Any Foundation
authorized by this Constitution must be a **consequence** of principles, boundaries, and explicit
domain elimination — not a premise used to justify them. If no domain survives elimination, epoch
pause is the lawful constitutional outcome.

**Ratification Standard.** A Founder or future architect SHALL be able to read this Constitution
and conclude whether **any** third external-capability descriptive domain remains admissible after
Runtime Foundation completion, or whether the descriptive External Capability Expansion epoch is
constitutionally complete. Article VII exists to make that conclusion auditable.

**Governance Cycle 6 Record.** This draft is produced under Gate B authorization recorded in
`docs/governance/GOVERNANCE-CYCLE-6-GATE-B-AUTHORIZATION.md`. Gate B authorizes drafting only.
Ratification is a separate Founder gate.

## Article II — Definitions

**Internal Cognitive Foundations**  
As defined in GAR-0017.

**External Systems**  
As defined in GAR-0017.

**Interface Foundation**

**Integration Foundation**  
The completed foundation governing External Integration, as authorized by GAR-0018 and realized by

**Runtime Foundation**  
The completed foundation governing External Runtime Governance, as authorized by GAR-0019 and
realized by ADR-0013 and GAR-SPRINT-0012 at release `v0.12.0-alpha`.

**External Capability Class**  
A category of external-facing architectural responsibility requiring independent constitutional
authority under GAR-0017 Article VI §4.

**Candidate Domain**  
A proposed External Capability Class or logically adjacent pattern evaluated for constitutional
admissibility under Articles V and VI before any Foundation is authorized.

**Admissible Domain**  
A Candidate Domain that survives elimination in Article VII §2.

**Operational Orchestration**  
Orchestration behavior that coordinates operational execution, process sequencing, dependency
resolution across runtime or integration participants, scheduling of multi-step external behavior,
or orchestration engine semantics. Operational Orchestration is prohibited at this expansion without
further constitutional authority beyond descriptive scope.

**External Orchestration Governance**  
The architectural discipline governing how external-facing orchestration contexts are described,
classified, bounded, and governed as orchestration participants **through** the Runtime Foundation,
Integration Foundation, and Constitutional Membrane — without Operational Orchestration, transport,
scheduling engines, or provider implementation.

**Foundation**  
As defined in GAR-0017.

**Constitutional Epoch (External Capability Expansion)**  
The constitutional period governing sequential introduction of external-capability Foundations beyond
the Interface Foundation. This epoch began with GAR-0018 and continues under this Constitution until
this Constitution declares descriptive completion or a fundamentally different class of expansion is
 separately ratified.

**Descriptive External-Capability Stack**  
The lawful ordered stack of descriptive external-capability Foundations at `v0.12.0-alpha`:

```
Interface Foundation → Integration Foundation → Runtime Foundation
```

**Epoch Pause (Descriptive Layer)**  
The constitutional state in which no additional descriptive external-capability Foundation is
admissible under current law, and the institution awaits separate constitutional evolution before
operational or new-epoch expansion.

**Constitutional Authority**  
As defined in GAR-0017.

Terms specific to any authorized Foundation are defined in Article IX only upon successful derivation
in Article VII §3. Under the derivation conclusion of this draft, no new Foundation terms are
activated.

## Article IV — Constitutional Authority

**§1.** GAR Constitutions establish the highest authority in Project Garuda.

**§2.** This Constitution derives authority from GAR-0019, GAR-0018, and GAR-0017 and SHALL NOT
contradict, supersede, or diminish any provision of GAR-0019, GAR-0018, GAR-0017, or any completed
Internal Cognitive Foundation.

**§3.** No ADR, sprint specification, mission, implementation, or repository artifact SHALL create,
extend, or infer constitutional authority beyond that granted by this Constitution and its
constitutional predecessors.

**§4. Constitutional Invariant 001 — Authority Flows Downward Only**

As established in GAR-0017.

**§5.** Skipping any stage in the constitutional introduction process SHALL NOT be permitted.

**§6. Relationship to GAR-0019**

GAR-0019 authorized the Runtime Foundation as the sole second external-capability expansion beyond
the Integration Foundation and declared that orchestration and all other external capabilities
require **independent constitutional authority** not inferable from GAR-0019 alone. Runtime
Foundation completion does not automatically authorize a third foundation. The lawful outcome of
the third derivation SHALL be determined only through Article VII — not assumed in this Article.

**§7. Relationship to GAR-0016**

Introduction of an entirely new Foundation constitutes constitutional expansion, not constitutional
amendment. The GAR-0016 Architecture Change Proposal process is **not** the lawful path for
authorizing a Foundation governed by this Constitution unless this Constitution explicitly fails to
identify an admissible domain and a separate constitutional evolution is separately ratified.
GAR-0016 remains reserved for genuine amendments to existing constitutional law.

**§8. Interpretive Boundary on Runtime Re-derivation Precedent**

GAR-0019 re-derived Runtime by distinguishing **Operational Runtime** from **External Runtime
Governance** as two interpretations of the GAR-0017-named runtime class at the post-Integration
epoch. That precedent authorizes **re-analysis** of a named class. It does **not** guarantee that
every GAR-0017-named class admits a descriptive interpretation. Each class SHALL be tested
independently under Article VII.

## Article V — Constitutional Principles Governing the Third Derivation

All boundaries, candidate elimination, conclusions, and any Foundation authorization SHALL derive
exclusively from these principles.

**§1. Membrane Supremacy**  
The Constitutional Membrane SHALL remain exclusive.

**§2. Sequential External Capability Introduction**  
External capabilities beyond the completed Runtime Foundation SHALL be introduced through discrete
Foundations, each authorized by independent constitutional authority, one at a time, with full
governance before implementation — **if** an admissible domain survives Article VII.

**§3. Descriptive Before Operational**  
Any third authorized Foundation SHALL govern **architectural description** only. It SHALL NOT
introduce Operational Orchestration, Operational Runtime, transport, scheduling, connectivity
operations, or provider invocation.

This principle is preserved as a constitutional invariant unless this Constitution demonstrates,
through explicit constitutional reasoning in Article VII, that governing principles must evolve.
No such evolution is asserted in Articles I through VI. Article VII tests descriptive admissibility
before any operational authority could exist.

**§4. Technology Neutrality Continuation**  
Any third authorized Foundation SHALL remain independent of all external technologies.

**§5. Additive Evolution**  
This derivation SHALL extend Project Garuda additively without redefining GAR-0019, GAR-0018,
GAR-0017, the Interface Foundation, the Integration Foundation, the Runtime Foundation, or any
Internal Cognitive Foundation except through GAR-0016.

**§6. Cognitive Separation Continuation**  
Any third authorized Foundation SHALL NOT perform cognition.

**§7. Variability Containment Extension**  
External variability SHALL continue to terminate at constitutionally authorized external-capability
Foundations.

**§8. Governance Before Repository**  
Every new capability SHALL enter through governance before the repository.

**§9. Stack Traversal**  
Any third authorized Foundation SHALL operate **through** the Interface Foundation, Integration
Foundation, and Runtime Foundation. It SHALL NOT bypass, duplicate, or weaken any completed
external-capability Foundation.

**§10. First Gap After Runtime**  
Any third authorized Foundation SHALL address the **first External Capability Class or logically
distinct descriptive domain** that:

1. Is not yet constitutionally governed after Runtime Foundation completion,
2. Is logically subsequent to Runtime Foundation establishment in the descriptive external-capability
   stack,
3. Does not require capabilities prohibited by Article VI §2,
4. Satisfies Descriptive Before Operational (§3),
5. Is analyzable as a **distinct descriptive layer** rather than a restatement of Integration
   relationship semantics or Runtime context classification semantics,
6. Does not presuppose Operational Orchestration to possess constitutional meaning.

If no domain satisfies all six conditions simultaneously, the lawful outcome is epoch pause under
Article IX — not forced foundation introduction.

**§11. Non-Redundancy**  
A Candidate Domain SHALL NOT be admissible if its descriptive scope is fully subsumed by the
Integration Foundation, Runtime Foundation, or Interface Foundation without introducing a
constitutionally distinct External Capability Class.

**§12. Orchestration Re-derivation Discipline**  
Re-analysis of Orchestration SHALL distinguish Operational Orchestration from External Orchestration
Governance. Admission of External Orchestration Governance requires the same standard of proof
applied to External Runtime Governance in GAR-0019: a non-vacuous descriptive domain that survives
§3 and §10 without constitutional evolution of Principle §3.

## Article VI — Constitutional Boundaries

**§1. Immutable Predecessors**

The following SHALL NOT be modified:

- GAR-0017, GAR-0018, GAR-0019 and all provisions therein
- All Internal Cognitive Foundations (Phase I)
- The Interface Foundation and Constitutional Membrane semantics
- The Integration Foundation and External Integration semantics
- The Runtime Foundation and External Runtime Governance semantics
- Platform Core object identity, validation, and serialization law

**§2. Prohibited Capabilities Without Further Constitutional Authority**

This derivation SHALL NOT introduce:

- Network protocols, APIs, HTTP, REST, gRPC, WebSockets
- Databases, persistence, message queues
- Authentication, authorization, identity systems
- LLM providers, MCP, or any specific external technology
- Runtime execution engines
- Orchestration engines
- Scheduling systems
- Workflow engines
- Deployment, containers, or cloud platforms
- Transport layers or provider implementations
- Operational integration execution, operational runtime invocation, or operational orchestration

These prohibitions inherit from GAR-0017 Article VI §2, GAR-0018 Article VI §2, and GAR-0019
Article VI §2.

**§3. Foundation Authorization Outcomes**

This Constitution admits **exactly one** of the following outcomes after Article VII:

1. Authorization of **exactly one** new Foundation, or
2. Authorization of **zero** additional Foundations and declaration of **epoch pause** at the
   descriptive external-capability layer.

Both outcomes are constitutionally valid. Outcome selection SHALL be determined exclusively by
Article VII elimination — not by institutional preference for continued expansion.

**§4. Membrane and Stack Exclusivity Preservation**

All external-capability Foundations SHALL operate through lawful stack traversal.

**§5. Epoch Designation**

This Constitution continues the **External Capability Expansion** epoch. It does not designate
Phase III. Descriptive completion of the epoch is not Phase III.

**§6. Rejected Architectural Alternatives Remain in Force**

Architectural alternatives rejected by ADR-0011, ADR-0012, and ADR-0013 remain constitutionally
rejected.

**§7. Sprint Scope Boundary**

No sprint is authorized by this Constitution. If a future Foundation is authorized elsewhere, its
implementing sprint SHALL be a new foundation sprint — not an enhancement sprint for Interface,
Integration, or Runtime.

authority beyond descriptive scope.

Candidate B — External Orchestration Governance (descriptive)
        ↓ REJECTED
External Orchestration Governance is the descriptive reinterpretation candidate analogous to
External Runtime Governance in GAR-0019. It is analyzed under Article V §12 at the post-Runtime
epoch.

Rejection grounds:

1. Non-Redundancy (Principle §11): The proposed descriptive scope — coordination intent metadata,
   participant orchestration contracts, sequencing descriptors subordinate to runtime and integration
   contracts — does not introduce a constitutionally distinct layer. Integration Foundation already
   governs external integration relationship semantics. Runtime Foundation already governs external
   runtime context classification, boundary traversal, and descriptive registry semantics. External
   Orchestration Governance would restate or compose semantics already distributed across the
   completed descriptive stack rather than address a first gap after Runtime.

2. Descriptive Before Operational (Principle §3): Orchestration's minimum constitutional meaning
   requires cross-participant sequencing or coordination affordances. Unlike External Runtime
   Governance — which describes bounded runtime context metadata prior to execution — orchestration
   descriptions that remain purely descriptive either (a) collapse into Integration relationship or
   Runtime classification semantics, or (b) smuggle operational sequencing semantics under
   descriptive labels. Neither outcome satisfies §3 without vacuity or operational contamination.

3. First Gap After Runtime (Principle §10): No first descriptive gap remains after Runtime
   Foundation completion for a distinct orchestration class. GAR-0017 naming of orchestration as an
   External Capability Class establishes candidacy for analysis — not admissibility. Naming does not
   override §10 and §11.

4. Distinction failure vs Runtime precedent: GAR-0019 admitted External Runtime Governance because
   runtime context description was logically subsequent to Integration, non-redundant, and
   constitutionally meaningful without operational execution. External Orchestration Governance lacks
   an analogous non-redundant descriptive domain at the post-Runtime epoch.

5. GAR-0019 scope exclusion continuity: GAR-0019 Article VIII §2 excluded orchestration of external
