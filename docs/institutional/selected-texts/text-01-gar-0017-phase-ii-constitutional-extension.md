## Editorial Introduction

Every constitutional journey has a point beyond which an institution begins to understand itself differently.

For Project Garuda, GAR-0017 represents that moment.

With this constitutional extension, the institution entered what later became known as the **descriptive external-capability epoch**. The Interface Foundation was not simply another technical capability. It established the constitutional recognition that interaction with the external environment deserved its own explicit institutional legitimacy.

Viewed from the conclusion of the First Constitutional Epoch, GAR-0017 now occupies a unique place within the constitutional corpus.

It is the first step in a sequence that continued through GAR-0018 and GAR-0019 before reaching its constitutional evaluation in GAR-0020.

Readers should approach this text not as an isolated constitutional artifact, but as the opening movement of a larger constitutional narrative.

The constitutional text that follows is reproduced exactly as ratified.

No editorial amendments, reinterpretations, or modernizations are introduced within the constitutional text itself.

## Article I — Purpose

This Constitution establishes the constitutional authority governing **Phase II** of Project Garuda.

Phase II extends the Garuda Operating System beyond its completed Internal Cognitive Foundations by defining the principles that shall forever govern interaction between Garuda and External Systems.

This Constitution authorizes the **Interface Foundation** as the sole constitutional boundary for such interaction.

This Constitution authorizes **architecture and governance only**. It does not authorize implementation, infrastructure, or external technology integration.

---

## Article II — Definitions

The following terms have constitutional meaning throughout Project Garuda.

**Internal Cognitive Foundations**  
The completed foundational layers constituting the Garuda Cognitive Operating System, comprising Platform Core, Memory, Knowledge, Context, Reasoning, Decision, Action, and Execution.

**External Systems**  
Any software, hardware, service, protocol, network, user interface, computational provider, or future technology operating outside the constitutional boundary of the Garuda Cognitive Operating System.

**Constitutional Membrane**  
The exclusive governance boundary separating External Systems from the Internal Cognitive Foundations.

**Interface Foundation**  
The foundational layer constitutionally responsible for governing all communication across the Constitutional Membrane.

**Foundation**  
A constitutionally authorized architectural layer possessing clearly defined responsibilities, dependency rules, certification requirements, and governance within the Garuda Operating System.

**Phase II**  
The constitutional epoch of Project Garuda beginning with the Interface Foundation and governed by this Constitution.

**Constitutional Authority**  
Authority established exclusively by approved GAR Constitutions. Constitutional authority cannot be inferred, extended, or created by any lower artifact.

**Architecture Decision Record (ADR)**  
A document that records an architectural decision derived from and subordinate to constitutional authority.

**Sprint Specification**  
An approved document that operationalizes architectural decisions into authorized implementation work. Sprint specifications never create constitutional authority.

## Article IV — Constitutional Authority

**§1.** GAR Constitutions establish the highest authority in Project Garuda.

**§2.** Phase II SHALL be governed by this Constitution upon its approval and ratification.

**§3.** No ADR, sprint specification, mission, implementation, or repository artifact SHALL create, extend, or infer constitutional authority.

**§4. Constitutional Invariant 001 — Authority Flows Downward Only**

Authority SHALL flow downward only:

```
GAR Constitution
        ↓
Architecture Decision Record
        ↓
Sprint Specification
        ↓
Mission Authorization
        ↓
Implementation
```

**§5.** Skipping any stage in the constitutional introduction process SHALL NOT be permitted.

---

## Article V — Fundamental Principles

**§1. Membrane Integrity**  
All communication between External Systems and Internal Cognitive Foundations SHALL occur exclusively through the Constitutional Membrane governed by the Interface Foundation.

**§2. Cognitive Purity**  
Internal Cognitive Foundations SHALL remain independent of External Systems. No Internal Cognitive Foundation SHALL depend upon any External System.

**§3. External Isolation**  
No External System SHALL access Internal Cognitive Foundations directly.

**§4. Determinism at the Boundary**  
All interaction crossing the Constitutional Membrane SHALL be governed by deterministic, technology-agnostic constitutional contracts before entering the Internal Cognitive Foundations.

**§5. Additive Evolution**  
Phase II SHALL extend Phase I. Phase II SHALL NOT redefine, replace, or invalidate any completed Internal Cognitive Foundation except as separately authorized through the Architecture Change Proposal process defined in GAR-0016.

**§6. Immutability of Identifiers**  
Once published, constitutional identifiers SHALL NOT be reused, renumbered, repurposed, or reassigned. Repository history forms part of the constitutional record.

**§7. Governance Before Repository Principle**  
Every new capability SHALL enter the system through governance before it enters the repository.

---

## Article VI — Phase II Scope

**§1. Authorized**  
Phase II constitutionally authorizes:

- The Constitutional Membrane
- The Interface Foundation
- Constitutional contracts governing external interaction
- Boundary validation governance
- Interface lifecycle governance
- Phase II dependency law as defined in Article VIII

**§2. Not Authorized**  
This Constitution does not authorize, and Phase II SHALL NOT introduce without separate constitutional authority:

- Network protocols, APIs, HTTP, REST, gRPC, WebSockets
- Databases, persistence, message queues
- Authentication, authorization, identity systems
- LLM providers, MCP, or any specific external technology
- Runtime execution, orchestration, or infrastructure
- Deployment, containers, or cloud platforms

**§3. Single Foundation**  
This Constitution authorizes one Phase II Foundation: the Interface Foundation. No additional Phase II Foundation is authorized by this document.

**§4. Future Extensions**  
Integration, runtime, orchestration, and all other external capabilities require independent constitutional authority. They SHALL NOT be inferred from this Constitution.

---

## Article VII — Interface Foundation

**§1. Recognition**  
The Interface Foundation is hereby constitutionally recognized as a Foundation within the meaning of Article II.

**§2. Boundary**  
The Interface Foundation SHALL constitute the exclusive constitutional boundary through which all communication between External Systems and the Internal Cognitive Foundations SHALL occur.

**§3. Constitutional Obligation**  
The Interface Foundation SHALL preserve the constitutional integrity of communication across the Constitutional Membrane.

**§4. Technology Neutrality**  
The Interface Foundation SHALL remain technology-agnostic. It SHALL NOT embody, prefer, or depend upon any specific External System or protocol.

**§5. Separation of Cognition**  
The Interface Foundation SHALL NOT perform cognition. Reasoning, memory, knowledge, context, decision, action, and execution descriptions remain the exclusive domain of the Internal Cognitive Foundations.

**§6. Variability Termination**  
All external variability SHALL terminate at the Interface Foundation. No external variability SHALL propagate into Internal Cognitive Foundations.

---

## Article VIII — Dependency Law

**§1. Direction**  
Dependencies SHALL flow only toward the Internal Cognitive Foundations.

**§2. Cognitive Independence**  
Internal Cognitive Foundations SHALL NOT depend upon External Systems or the Interface Foundation.

**§3. Interface Dependencies**  
The Interface Foundation MAY depend upon previously approved Internal Cognitive Foundations.

**§4. Prohibition of Circularity**  
Circular dependencies between Foundations, or between External Systems and Internal Cognitive Foundations, SHALL NOT be permitted.

**§5. Immutability**  
Unless separately authorized through the Architecture Change Proposal process defined in GAR-0016, completed Internal Cognitive Foundations SHALL remain immutable.

---

## Article IX — Repository Integrity

**§1. Repository Primacy**  
The committed repository SHALL remain the authoritative implementation record of Project Garuda.

**§2. Identifier Permanence**  
Constitutional identifiers are permanent.

**§3. Authority Derivation**  
Architectural identifiers SHALL derive authority exclusively from GAR Constitutions.

**§4. Sprint Authority**  
Sprint identifiers SHALL derive authority exclusively from approved ADRs and applicable GAR Constitutions.

**§5. Mission Authority**  
Mission identifiers SHALL derive authority exclusively from approved sprint specifications.

**§6. Documentation Conformance**  
Documentation that conflicts with committed repository state SHALL be corrected to reflect the repository, not the reverse.

---

## Article X — Governance

**§1. Foundation Introduction Rule**  
No sprint SHALL introduce a new Foundation unless its constitutional authority, inheritance model, certification strategy, and impact on previously completed Foundations have been explicitly documented, reviewed, and approved before implementation.

**§2. Engineering Handbook**  
Engineering governance documents SHALL implement this Constitution. They SHALL NOT contradict, supersede, or dilute constitutional authority.

**§3. Reviews**  
Architecture reviews SHALL verify constitutional conformance before mission completion.

**§4. Certification**  
Cross-foundation certification SHALL verify that new Foundations preserve all prior Foundations without modification unless separately authorized.

---

## Article XI — Compliance

**§1.** Any architecture, ADR, sprint specification, implementation, mission, or repository change inconsistent with this Constitution SHALL be considered constitutionally non-compliant.

**§2.** Constitutionally non-compliant work SHALL NOT be approved for implementation.

**§3.** Constitutionally non-compliant commits SHALL NOT be merged into the authoritative repository.

**§4.** Ambiguity regarding constitutional conformance SHALL halt work until clarified by the Chief Systems Architect and ratified by the Founder where required.

---

## Article XII — Activation

Phase II SHALL NOT enter implementation until all of the following exist and are approved:

1. GAR-0017 v1.0 (this Constitution, Founder ratified)
2. ADR-0011 — Interface Foundation
3. GAR-SPRINT-0010 — Approved sprint specification
4. Explicit Mission Alpha authorization

Until all conditions are satisfied, Phase II SHALL remain in Constitution Drafting.

Upon ratification of GAR-0017 v1.0, this Constitution SHALL:

- Activate Phase II under constitutional governance
- Authorize creation of ADR-0011
- Authorize revision and approval of GAR-SPRINT-0010
- Preserve immutability of Phase I
- Establish the Constitutional Membrane as permanent architectural law

## Article XIII — Amendments

**§1.** GAR-0017 MAY be amended only through the Architecture Change Proposal process defined in GAR-0016.

**§2.** No amendment SHALL reduce the immutability protections of Phase I Internal Cognitive Foundations without explicit constitutional authorization.

**§3.** GAR-0016 SHALL remain the permanent process governing all constitutional change, including amendments to this document.

2. Preservation of completed Foundations
3. Additive evolution over modification
4. Repository determinism
5. Explicit governance over inferred intent

## Historical Context

GAR-0017 addressed a constitutional question that had not previously been resolved within Project Garuda.

The institution required an explicit constitutional understanding of its relationship with the external environment before architectural interpretation or engineering implementation could proceed responsibly.

By establishing the Interface Foundation, GAR-0017 answered that question and created the constitutional conditions for subsequent exploration of Integration and Runtime.

Its significance therefore extends beyond the foundation it introduced.

It established the constitutional pattern through which the remainder of the descriptive external-capability epoch would unfold.

---

## Lasting Influence

The influence of GAR-0017 can be observed throughout the First Constitutional Epoch.

Among its enduring contributions were:

- the constitutional recognition of Interface as a distinct institutional concern,
- the establishment of the first foundation within the descriptive external-capability epoch,
- reinforcement of Authority Before Action through constitutional sequencing,
- the constitutional framework upon which GAR-0018 and GAR-0019 were later constructed,
- the opening constitutional movement of the sequence ultimately evaluated in GAR-0020.

Although later constitutional artifacts expanded the institution's descriptive understanding, none replaced the constitutional role established by GAR-0017.

Its authority remains part of the institution's enduring constitutional baseline.
