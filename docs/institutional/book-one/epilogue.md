This epilogue creates no constitutional, architectural, sprint, or engineering authority.

---

Every institution begins in uncertainty.

Someone asks a question that has never been answered before.
Someone imagines that things could be arranged differently.
Someone has the patience to test that belief against reality.

Most such attempts disappear with time.
Some succeed for a while.
A very small number leave behind something more enduring than their creators.

They leave behind a way of thinking.

Project Garuda began with a question about intelligent systems.
It concluded its first constitutional epoch with a lesson about institutions.

Ideas become stronger when they are governed before they are implemented.
Authority becomes trustworthy when it is recorded rather than assumed.
History becomes meaningful when it preserves not only conclusions, but the reasoning that produced them.
And stewardship begins when one generation writes carefully enough that the next can understand before it changes.

The pages of this book describe one institution, at one moment in its history.

They do not claim to offer the only path.
They do not ask every future institution to resemble Garuda.

They offer something simpler.

An invitation.

If these chapters encourage even one future steward to pause before acting...
To ask one better question before accepting an easy answer...
To preserve one important piece of reasoning that might otherwise have been forgotten...

Then the first constitutional epoch will have reached beyond the boundaries of its own repository.

Institutions, like people, are remembered not only for what they create.
They are remembered for what they choose to preserve.

The first constitutional epoch is complete.
The institution remains.

Its future now belongs to those who inherit it.

May they inherit it wisely.
