# The First Edition Editorial Pass

## Project Garuda Preservation Series

### Editorial Charter

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Document Type | Institutional Literature |
| Authority | Informational (Non-Constitutional) |
| Version | 1.0 |

This document creates no constitutional, architectural, sprint, or engineering authority.

---

The completion of the Preservation Series marks the conclusion of the institution's first comprehensive preservation effort.

The purpose of the First Edition Editorial Pass is not to alter institutional meaning, constitutional authority, architectural interpretation, governance history, or historical record.

Its purpose is to present the completed Preservation Series with the clarity, consistency, and craftsmanship appropriate for a permanent first edition.

Editorial refinement shall never modify institutional authority.

It shall only improve the presentation of that authority.

---

## Editorial Principles

### 1. Preserve Meaning

No editorial revision shall alter:

- constitutional intent,
- architectural interpretation,
- governance history,
- institutional chronology,
- historical conclusions,
- repository references.

Meaning is preserved exactly.

Only presentation may improve.

---

### 2. Preserve Canonical Authority

The repository remains the sole canonical source of institutional authority.

Where editorial convenience differs from repository fidelity, repository fidelity always prevails.

The Preservation Series explains the institution.

The repository governs it.

---

### 3. Improve Readability

The editorial pass may improve:

- typography,
- page rhythm,
- spacing,
- headings,
- figure placement,
- caption consistency,
- cross-references,
- indexes,
- tables,
- print layout.

Such refinements improve accessibility without altering substance.

---

### 4. Maintain a Unified Voice

Every volume should read as though prepared by the same editorial hand.

Narrative tone, terminology, formatting, citation style, and visual presentation should remain consistent across:

- Book One,
- Institutional Atlas,
- Companion & Glossary,
- Institutional Timeline,
- Selected Constitutional Texts.

Consistency strengthens institutional identity.

---

### 5. Distinguish Editorial Voice from Institutional Voice

Where editorial commentary appears, it should always remain clearly identifiable.

Editorial introductions provide context.

Institutional artifacts provide authority.

The distinction must never become ambiguous.

---

## Editorial Checklist

The First Edition Editorial Pass should include:

- terminology consistency,
- glossary cross-checks,
- constitutional citation verification,
- repository reference verification,
- illustration review,
- timeline consistency,
- chapter numbering validation,
- table formatting,
- page layout,
- front matter,
- back matter,
- index,
- bibliography,
- acknowledgements (if appropriate),
- print-ready formatting.

---

## Completion Criteria

The First Edition Editorial Pass is complete when:

- every preservation volume reads consistently,
- every repository reference has been verified,
- every cross-reference functions correctly,
- every illustration is finalized,
- no editorial inconsistencies remain,
- the complete Preservation Series is suitable for archival publication.

---

## Legacy

The First Edition Editorial Pass does not create new institutional knowledge.

It honors the knowledge that already exists.

Its responsibility is to ensure that future generations encounter the institution with the same clarity, care, and discipline through which it was originally preserved.

When complete, the First Edition will stand not merely as a collection of books, but as the institution's first enduring library.

That library is offered not as the final word of Project Garuda, but as the faithful inheritance of its First Constitutional Epoch.
