# Stewardship Principles

The Preservation Series exists to:

- improve understanding,
- preserve institutional history,
- strengthen continuity,
- support future stewards,
- increase navigability,
- protect institutional context.

It deliberately avoids creating new authority.

---

# The Preservation Cycle

```text
Create
     ↓
Record
     ↓
Preserve
     ↓
Explain
     ↓
Teach
     ↓
Inherit
```

Every generation contributes to institutional memory.
Every generation also benefits from the stewardship of those who came before.

---

# Reading This Plate

The Preservation Series is the bridge between governance and inheritance.

Constitutions establish legitimacy.
Architecture establishes structure.
Engineering establishes implementation.

Preservation ensures that future generations can understand all three before attempting to change them.

# Key Insight

The Preservation Series reflects the final lesson of the First Constitutional Epoch.

Building an institution is only the beginning.

An institution endures because each generation chooses to preserve its reasoning with the same care that earlier generations used to create it.

The Institutional Atlas is therefore not merely a collection of diagrams.
It is an invitation.

An invitation to understand before acting, to preserve before extending, and to inherit before attempting to transform.

Every future steward begins here.

Not with authority.
But with understanding.
