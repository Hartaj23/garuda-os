This plate illustrates the constitutional principle of **Descriptive Before Operational**.

# The Constitutional Principle

The descriptive domain explains the institution.
The operational domain directs the institution.

These are different constitutional concerns.

The First Constitutional Epoch deliberately remained within the descriptive domain.

Operational questions were neither assumed nor implicitly authorized.

---

# Why the Separation Matters

Without this distinction, descriptive governance can gradually become operational governance.

The institution therefore maintained a clear constitutional boundary.

Description establishes understanding.
Operation establishes behaviour.

Understanding must exist before behaviour can be governed responsibly.

---

# Constitutional Evaluation

During Governance Cycle 6, several potential directions beyond Runtime were examined.

The institution asked:

- Does this candidate describe a genuinely new constitutional concern?
- Or does it introduce operational behaviour?

Where operational meaning became inseparable from the proposal, constitutional admission was not justified within the descriptive epoch.

---

# Relationship to GAR-0020

GAR-0020 did not reject operational governance.

GAR-0020 concluded something more precise.

Under the governing constitutional principles of the First Constitutional Epoch, no further **descriptive** external-capability foundation remained admissible.

The descriptive layer therefore reached constitutional completion.

# Key Insight

Project Garuda distinguishes between describing an institution and directing its behaviour.

The First Constitutional Epoch completed the descriptive external-capability layer without assuming that operational governance should automatically follow.

This distinction allowed the institution to recognize constitutional completion without confusing it with technological limitation.

The result was not the end of possibility.
It was the disciplined recognition that every new constitutional domain must first earn its legitimacy before becoming part of the institution.
