# Constitutional Progression

## Interface

The institution established the constitutional boundary between Project Garuda and the external environment.

Interaction became a distinct descriptive concern.

## Integration

The institution recognized that relationships among external capabilities constituted a separate descriptive concern.

Integration clarified the external capability landscape without redefining Interface.

## Runtime

The institution established the descriptive understanding of external capability state.

Runtime completed the descriptive model of external-capability governance.

## GAR-0020

The institution examined whether another descriptive foundation remained constitutionally admissible.

The conclusion was that no further descriptive external-capability foundation satisfied the governing constitutional principles.

The outcome was an epoch pause rather than further expansion.

# Why the Sequence Ends

The endpoint of this diagram is deliberate.

It does not continue into another foundation.

This is not an omission.
It is the constitutional conclusion reached through Governance Cycle 6 and ratified in GAR-0020.

The descriptive external-capability layer is complete under the current constitutional law.

Future evolution, if justified, begins through a new governance cycle rather than by extending this sequence.

This diagram should not be interpreted as an implementation roadmap.

# Key Insight

The evolution of the descriptive external-capability layer demonstrates one of the defining principles of Constitutional Engineering.

Constitutional expansion is justified only while genuine constitutional questions remain unanswered.

When those questions have been answered, disciplined governance recognizes completion instead of pursuing expansion for its own sake.

The strength of the institution lies not in how many foundations it creates, but in how faithfully it distinguishes necessity from possibility.
