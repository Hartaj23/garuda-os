## Purpose

This plate illustrates the complete lifecycle of a Governance Cycle within Project Garuda.

A Governance Cycle is the institution's formal process for evaluating, authorizing, and, where appropriate, adopting constitutional evolution.

Every cycle begins and ends in Institutional HOLD.

# Governance Stages

## 1. Institutional HOLD

The institution is stable.

No constitutional, architectural, sprint, or engineering work is authorized unless explicitly opened through governance.

## 2. Institutional Assessment

The repository is reviewed as the canonical institutional record.

Current constitutional, architectural, and governance baselines are evaluated.

No drafting occurs during assessment.

## 3. Gate A — Founder Decision

The Founder determines whether constitutional evolution is necessary.

This decision concerns institutional direction only.
It does not authorize drafting.

## 4. Institutional Recommendation

If constitutional evolution is required, institutional assessment recommends the appropriate governance artifact.

Recommendations identify a path.
They do not authorize work.

## 5. Gate B — Founder Authorization

The Founder explicitly authorizes (or declines to authorize) drafting of the recommended governance artifact.

Authorization is specific to the named artifact.
No additional authority is implied.

## 6. Governance Draft

The authorized governance artifact is prepared.

It remains a proposal until ratified.

Drafting does not alter the constitutional baseline.

## 7. Founder Ratification

The Founder may:

- ratify,
- request revisions,
- or decline the proposal.

Only ratification changes the constitutional baseline.

## 8. Governance Cycle Closure

The cycle concludes after governance objectives have been completed.

Closure records institutional outcomes and returns the institution to Institutional HOLD.

---

# Possible Outcomes

A Governance Cycle may conclude with different legitimate outcomes.

Examples include:

- constitutional extension,
- constitutional clarification,
- governance refinement,
- documentation improvement,
- epoch pause,
- or no constitutional change.

Success is measured by disciplined governance, not by the quantity of new artifacts.

# Fundamental Principles

```text
Assessment
      ↓
Decision
      ↓
Recommendation
      ↓
Authorization
      ↓
Draft
      ↓
Ratification
      ↓
Closure
      ↓
Institutional HOLD
```

Each transition requires explicit authority.

No stage automatically authorizes the next.

---

# Governance Characteristics

| Characteristic | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Repository-first | Decisions are grounded in committed institutional evidence |
| Explicit authority | Every governance transition is separately authorized |
| Traceable history | Every significant event is recorded |
| Separation of roles | Decision, recommendation, authorization, drafting, and ratification remain distinct |
| Return to HOLD | Every cycle concludes in institutional stability |

---

# Reading This Plate

A Governance Cycle is not a project plan.

It is the institution's method for examining whether constitutional evolution is justified.

The cycle may produce new constitutional authority, refine existing governance, or conclude that no institutional change is presently warranted.

In every case, the institution returns to Institutional HOLD with a clearer understanding of its own state.

---

# Key Insight

The Governance Cycle is the heartbeat of Project Garuda.

It ensures that institutional evolution occurs through evidence, explicit authority, and recorded reasoning rather than assumption or momentum.

Every cycle begins with a question.
Every cycle ends with a more mature institution.
