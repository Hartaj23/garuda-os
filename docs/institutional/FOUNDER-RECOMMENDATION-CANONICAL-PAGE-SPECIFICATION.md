## Why Canonical Page Specification Matters

Design Language stated:

*If the logo were removed, the page should still unmistakably feel like Project Garuda.*

Everything necessary to **prove** that now exists — without a logo, without branding graphics.

Just one page.

The page itself becomes the test.

---

## Canonical Page Specification Scope

This document would define, in measurable detail:

- page width
- margins
- reading rhythm
- heading proportions
- paragraph spacing
- blockquote treatment
- repository references
- diagram placement
- tables
- navigation rhythm
- whitespace philosophy

In other words: **What does a Garuda page actually look like?**

Not in theory.

In measurable detail.

---

## Sequence Rationale

Only after that page exists should the logo be designed.

Then the question becomes:

Not *"What logo should Garuda have?"*

But *"What mark belongs naturally on a Garuda page?"*

That is a completely different design problem — and a better one.

---

## Design Tokens Assessment

The token palette — warm paper, ink, institutional slate, archive brown — does not feel like software.

It feels like scholarship.

Exactly as intended.

No change recommended.

---

## Proposed Founder Decision

When issued, the next Founder Decision should read:

**Mission 001 — Phase B**

**Authorize:** Canonical Page Specification v1.0

**Not authorized:**

- Logo
- CSS
- Implementation

**Scope:** One page. One canonical expression of the entire visual philosophy.

If that page can stand on its own, everything else becomes dramatically easier.

---

## Institutional Identity

Years from now, people may not remember Garuda's first logo.

They may remember opening one of its documents and thinking:

*"I've never seen a page that feels quite like this."*

That is identity that survives redesigns.

That is **institutional identity** — not brand identity.

Garuda has earned exactly that.
