## Definition

**Constitutional Engineering** is the institutional discipline through which Project Garuda establishes, evaluates, evolves, and preserves constitutional authority before architecture, engineering, or implementation.

Within Project Garuda, Constitutional Engineering is concerned with institutional legitimacy rather than technical construction.

It asks whether a capability deserves to exist before asking how it should be designed or implemented.

Constitutional Engineering therefore governs the evolution of the institution itself.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of Constitutional Engineering is to ensure that every significant act of institutional creation is supported by explicit authority, careful reasoning, and a traceable governance process.

Rather than allowing projects to evolve through informal convention or accumulated momentum, Constitutional Engineering establishes a disciplined sequence:

- determine institutional necessity,
- establish constitutional authority,
- interpret constitutional intent,
- authorize implementation,
- preserve institutional reasoning.

This sequence protects the long-term coherence of the institution.

---

## Core Principles

### Governance Before Architecture

Institutional legitimacy is established before architectural interpretation begins.

Architecture faithfully interprets constitutional intent.
It does not create constitutional authority.

### Authority Before Action

Every meaningful act of creation must inherit explicit authority from an authorized predecessor.

Authority is recorded.
It is never assumed.

### Repository-First

The repository serves as the institution's canonical memory.

Institutional reasoning becomes authoritative only when preserved through the appropriate governance process.

### Descriptive Before Operational

The institution first establishes descriptive constitutional understanding before evaluating operational domains.

This principle guided the First Constitutional Epoch and informed the constitutional conclusion reached in GAR-0020.

### Stewardship Across Generations

Constitutional Engineering is designed to preserve institutional continuity beyond the participation of any individual contributor.

Future generations inherit both the institution's conclusions and the reasoning that produced them.

## Common Misunderstandings

### "Constitutional Engineering is software architecture."

Incorrect.

Architecture is one stage within the institutional lifecycle.

Constitutional Engineering begins before architecture and continues beyond implementation through governance and stewardship.

### "Constitutional Engineering creates constitutions."

Only in part.

It also evaluates whether new constitutions are justified, refines governance, preserves institutional memory, and recognizes when constitutional expansion should not occur.

### "Constitutional Engineering always produces new artifacts."

No.

One of its defining characteristics is that it accepts every legitimate outcome supported by evidence.

Those outcomes may include constitutional extension, clarification, refinement, preservation, or the recognition that no further constitutional change is presently justified.

## Key Insight

Constitutional Engineering is not primarily concerned with building software.
It is concerned with building institutions capable of governing software with legitimacy, clarity, and continuity.

Its greatest achievement is not the number of constitutions it produces.
Its greatest achievement is the discipline to determine, through evidence and explicit authority, whether another constitution should exist at all.

That discipline transforms governance from a supporting activity into the enduring foundation upon which the institution itself is built.
