## Definition

The **Architectural Corpus** is the complete body of approved architectural records that interpret constitutional authority into coherent institutional structure.

Within Project Garuda, architectural records explain how constitutional intent should be understood, organized, and translated into enduring foundations.

The Architectural Corpus does not establish constitutional legitimacy.
It faithfully interprets constitutionally ratified authority into architectural form.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of the Architectural Corpus is to preserve a clear separation between constitutional authority and engineering implementation.

Constitutions answer **what** the institution authorizes.
Architecture explains **how** that authority should be interpreted before implementation begins.

This distinction ensures that engineering proceeds from a coherent architectural understanding rather than from direct interpretation of constitutional artifacts.

---

## Architectural Characteristics

### Interpretive Authority

Architecture derives its legitimacy from the Constitutional Corpus.

It interprets constitutional intent without extending, amending, or replacing constitutional authority.

### Structural Coherence

Architectural records establish consistent relationships among institutional foundations.

Each architectural decision contributes to a coherent and comprehensible institutional structure.

### Foundation Definition

The Architectural Corpus identifies the boundaries, responsibilities, and interactions of approved foundations.

These definitions guide future engineering work while remaining faithful to constitutional intent.

### Stable Interpretation

Architectural records provide a stable interpretive layer between governance and engineering.

Changes to architecture require their own explicit governance and authorization.

## Common Misunderstandings

### "Architecture creates institutional authority."

Incorrect.

Institutional authority originates within the Constitutional Corpus.
Architecture derives its legitimacy by interpreting that authority faithfully.

### "Engineering can replace architectural interpretation."

No.

Engineering implements architecture.
It does not redefine architectural intent.

If architectural understanding becomes insufficient, the appropriate governance process must authorize architectural evolution.

### "Architecture is permanent."

Not necessarily.

Architectural records may evolve through explicitly authorized governance.
Their authority remains interpretive rather than constitutional.

## Reading This Concept

The Architectural Corpus should be understood as an interpretive discipline rather than a design catalogue.

Its purpose is not simply to describe systems.
Its purpose is to ensure that constitutional intent is translated into coherent architectural understanding before implementation begins.

This protects engineering from ambiguity while preserving constitutional integrity.

---

## Key Insight

The Architectural Corpus demonstrates that interpretation is itself an institutional responsibility.

By separating constitutional legitimacy from architectural understanding, Project Garuda ensures that no participant is required to infer architectural meaning directly from constitutional authority.

Architecture therefore serves as the institution's disciplined interpreter.

It preserves fidelity to constitutional intent while providing the clarity required for responsible engineering and long-term institutional continuity.
