## Definition

A **Governance Cycle** is the institution's formal process for evaluating whether constitutional evolution is necessary and, where appropriate, guiding that evolution through explicit authority, disciplined governance, and recorded institutional reasoning.

Each Governance Cycle is a bounded institutional event.

It begins with Institutional HOLD.
It concludes by returning the institution to Institutional HOLD.

A Governance Cycle does not exist to produce constitutional change.
It exists to determine whether constitutional change is justified.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of a Governance Cycle is to protect institutional legitimacy.

Rather than allowing constitutional evolution to occur continuously or informally, the Governance Cycle establishes a structured process through which new institutional questions are examined before any constitutional authority is created.

Every Governance Cycle therefore asks a simple but profound question:

**"Does the institution truly need to evolve?"**

Only after that question has been answered does the institution consider constitutional drafting.

---

## Lifecycle

A Governance Cycle progresses through clearly defined institutional stages.

```text
Institutional HOLD
        ↓
Institutional Assessment
        ↓
Gate A
        ↓
Recommendation
        ↓
Gate B
        ↓
Drafting
        ↓
Ratification
        ↓
Closure
        ↓
Institutional HOLD
```

Each stage represents a distinct institutional responsibility.

No stage automatically authorizes the next.

---

## Characteristics

### Repository-First

Every Governance Cycle begins by examining the repository as the institution's canonical memory.

Institutional assessment relies upon committed evidence rather than personal recollection.

### Explicit Authority

Every governance transition requires its own authorization.

Decision, recommendation, authorization, drafting, ratification, and closure remain separate institutional acts.

### Bounded Scope

Each Governance Cycle addresses a specific constitutional question.

Its authority extends only to that question.

Completion of one cycle does not authorize future cycles.

### Recorded History

Every significant governance event becomes part of the Governance Corpus.

Future stewards inherit both the outcome and the reasoning that produced it.

## Common Misunderstandings

### "A Governance Cycle exists to create new constitutions."

No.

A Governance Cycle exists to evaluate institutional necessity.

A legitimate outcome may be constitutional extension, clarification, refinement, preservation, an epoch pause, or no constitutional change at all.

### "Every Governance Cycle produces architectural or engineering work."

Incorrect.

Architecture, sprint planning, and engineering require their own explicit authorizations.

A Governance Cycle may conclude without any downstream implementation activity.

### "Institutional HOLD means no governance is occurring."

Institutional HOLD is the resting posture between Governance Cycles.

It preserves institutional stability until a genuinely new constitutional question justifies opening another cycle.

## Reading This Concept

The Governance Cycle should be understood as the institution's method of constitutional self-examination.

It allows the institution to question itself before attempting to change itself.

This distinction is fundamental.

An institution that evolves without first examining its own necessity risks confusing movement with maturity.

The Governance Cycle prevents that confusion.

---

## Key Insight

The Governance Cycle is the institution's recurring act of disciplined reflection.

It ensures that constitutional evolution is always preceded by evidence, explicit authority, and careful institutional reasoning.

Every cycle begins with a question.
Not every cycle ends with a new constitution.

Its true success lies not in producing change, but in ensuring that whatever change does occur has genuinely earned its place within the institution.

That is why every Governance Cycle concludes by returning Project Garuda to Institutional HOLD.

The institution rests until the next question deserves to be asked.
