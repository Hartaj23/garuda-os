## Definition

**Repository-First** is the institutional principle that the repository is the canonical source of Project Garuda's authority, memory, and historical continuity.

Within Project Garuda, institutional legitimacy is established through committed repository artifacts rather than conversations, recollections, unpublished drafts, or informal agreement.

The repository is therefore the institution's primary record of what has been authorized, why it was authorized, and how that authority evolved over time.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of Repository-First is to ensure that institutional understanding survives beyond individual participants.

Projects often depend upon conversations, meetings, or personal recollection to explain important decisions.

Project Garuda deliberately avoids this dependence.

Instead, every significant constitutional, architectural, governance, engineering, and preservation milestone is recorded within the repository.

Future generations inherit evidence rather than memory alone.

---

## Core Principles

### Canonical Record

The repository is the authoritative institutional record.

Where differences arise between conversation and committed artifacts, the committed repository state governs.

### Recorded Authority

Institutional authority exists only when preserved through the appropriate governance process and committed to the repository.

Ideas discussed but not recorded remain proposals rather than institutional truth.

### Historical Continuity

The repository preserves the chronological development of the institution.

Every governance transition can be reconstructed through recorded evidence.

### Institutional Transparency

Future stewards should be able to understand institutional reasoning without requiring access to private conversations or personal explanation.

### Generational Independence

Repository-First allows the institution to remain understandable even after its original participants have departed.

The institution teaches itself through its preserved record.

## Common Misunderstandings

### "Repository-First means code comes before discussion."

Incorrect.

Discussion remains valuable.
It generates ideas, explores alternatives, and refines understanding.

Repository-First concerns what becomes institutional memory, not how ideas originate.

### "Everything discussed becomes institutional truth."

No.

Only artifacts that complete the appropriate governance process and are committed to the repository become part of the institution's canonical record.

Conversation inspires.
The repository preserves.

### "The repository replaces human judgment."

It does not.

The repository preserves the outcomes of institutional judgment.

Future participants continue to exercise judgment through the same governance processes established by the institution.

## Key Insight

Repository-First does not elevate files above people.
It preserves the reasoning of people so that future generations are not required to depend upon their continued presence.

The repository therefore becomes more than a technical archive.
It becomes the institution's memory, its evidence, and its bridge across generations.

Because of Repository-First, every future steward inherits not only what Project Garuda became, but also the reasoning that allowed it to become so.
