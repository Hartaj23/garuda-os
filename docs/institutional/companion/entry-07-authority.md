---

## Definition

Within Project Garuda, **authority** is the explicit institutional legitimacy that permits a specific action, decision, interpretation, or implementation to occur.

Authority is never presumed.
It is established through the institution's constitutional and governance processes, recorded within the repository, and exercised only within the scope for which it has been granted.

Authority therefore represents permission rather than capability.

The ability to perform an action does not imply the authority to perform it.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of authority is to preserve institutional legitimacy.

By requiring explicit authority before every meaningful act of creation, Project Garuda ensures that constitutional intent, architectural interpretation, governance activity, and engineering implementation remain coherent and traceable.

Authority protects the institution from accidental evolution driven by convenience, momentum, or assumption.

---

## Characteristics of Authority

### Explicit

Authority must be recorded.

Institutional legitimacy cannot depend upon implication or shared understanding alone.

### Scoped

Every authorization has defined boundaries.

Authority granted for one institutional activity does not automatically extend to another.

For example:

- authorization to draft is not authorization to ratify,
- ratification is not authorization to begin architecture,
- architectural approval is not authorization for engineering.

Each stage receives its own authority.

### Traceable

Every significant exercise of authority can be reconstructed through repository history.

Future stewards should always be able to determine:

- who granted authority,
- what was authorized,
- when it was authorized,
- why it was authorized.

### Revocable Through Governance

Authority remains subject to the institution's governance processes.

Future constitutional evolution may refine or replace existing authority through explicitly authorized institutional procedures.

## Common Misunderstandings

### "Capability creates authority."

Incorrect.

Capability describes what can be done.
Authority determines what may legitimately be done.

Project Garuda deliberately separates these concepts.

### "Once authority is granted, it applies everywhere."

No.

Authority is specific to the action for which it was granted.

Separate institutional acts require separate institutional authority.

### "Authority slows progress."

Authority slows ungoverned progress.

Its purpose is not delay.
Its purpose is legitimacy.

By ensuring that creation follows explicit institutional approval, authority protects long-term coherence even when it requires short-term patience.

## Reading This Concept

Authority should not be understood as hierarchy alone.

Within Project Garuda, authority is primarily a question of institutional legitimacy.

Every meaningful action asks the same question:

**"What explicitly authorizes this?"**

If that question cannot be answered through the repository, the authority chain is incomplete.

---

## Key Insight

Authority is the constitutional language through which Project Garuda governs itself.

It transforms decisions into legitimacy, legitimacy into interpretation, interpretation into implementation, and implementation into institutional memory.

Because authority is explicit, future generations inherit more than completed work.

They inherit confidence that every significant artifact earned its place within the institution through a visible and traceable chain of legitimacy.

That confidence is one of the institution's greatest strengths.
