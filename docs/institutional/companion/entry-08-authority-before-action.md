## Definition

**Authority Before Action** is the foundational governance principle of Project Garuda.

It states that every meaningful act of institutional creation must be preceded by explicit institutional authority.

No constitutional proposal, architectural interpretation, governance transition, sprint, engineering activity, or institutional publication that creates authority may proceed without first inheriting legitimacy from an authorized predecessor.

Authority therefore precedes action.
Action never creates its own authority.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of Authority Before Action is to preserve institutional legitimacy throughout every stage of institutional evolution.

Rather than relying upon capability, assumption, or momentum, Project Garuda requires each significant transition to demonstrate an explicit and traceable source of authority.

This principle ensures that institutional evolution remains disciplined, auditable, and faithful to constitutional intent.

---

## The Authority Chain

Every meaningful institutional act follows an unbroken chain of legitimacy.

```text
Question
      ↓
Assessment
      ↓
Decision
      ↓
Recommendation
      ↓
Authorization
      ↓
Draft
      ↓
Ratification
      ↓
Architecture
      ↓
Sprint
      ↓
Engineering
      ↓
Repository Memory
```

Each stage inherits authority from the stage immediately preceding it.

No transition may be inferred or skipped.

---

## Why the Principle Exists

Institutions naturally accumulate momentum.

Over time, participants may begin assuming that desirable work is therefore authorized work.

Authority Before Action deliberately rejects that assumption.

The institution asks a different question:

**What explicitly authorizes this next step?**

Only after that question has been answered does institutional work continue.

## Common Misunderstandings

### "Authority Before Action discourages initiative."

No.

It encourages disciplined initiative.

Ideas may emerge at any time.
Institutional action begins only after appropriate authority exists.

### "Authority Before Action requires excessive bureaucracy."

Incorrect.

The principle distinguishes necessary governance from unnecessary procedure.

Its purpose is clarity rather than complexity.

Every governance step exists because it answers a different institutional question.

### "Once authority exists, every future action is authorized."

No.

Authority remains scoped.

Each institutional transition requires its own explicit authorization.

Authority is inherited step by step rather than granted indefinitely.

## Reading This Concept

Authority Before Action should be understood as the institution's governing discipline rather than merely one governance rule among many.

Every constitutional extension, architectural interpretation, engineering foundation, governance refinement, and preservation activity ultimately reflects this principle.

It provides the rhythm through which the institution evolves responsibly.

---

## Key Insight

Authority Before Action transforms governance from a sequence of approvals into a philosophy of institutional responsibility.

It recognizes that capability is never sufficient on its own.
Legitimacy must always come first.

Because every meaningful artifact within Project Garuda can be traced through an explicit chain of authority, the institution preserves not only confidence in its conclusions, but confidence in the way those conclusions were reached.

That confidence is one of the defining characteristics of Constitutional Engineering itself.
