## Definition

An **Institutional Recommendation** is the formal conclusion of institutional assessment that identifies the governance artifact most appropriate for addressing a constitutionally recognized institutional question.

A recommendation expresses institutional judgment.
It does not create institutional authority.

Its purpose is to identify the recommended direction for Founder consideration following a positive Gate A decision.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of an Institutional Recommendation is to separate institutional analysis from institutional authorization.

Assessment determines what the evidence suggests.
The recommendation communicates that conclusion.

Whether work should begin remains a separate Founder decision.

This distinction preserves both analytical integrity and constitutional authority.

---

## Position Within the Governance Cycle

An Institutional Recommendation appears only after:

- institutional assessment has been completed,
- Gate A has determined that constitutional evolution is required.

It precedes Gate B.

```text
Institutional Assessment
            ↓
Gate A
            ↓
Institutional Recommendation
            ↓
Gate B
```

The recommendation bridges institutional understanding and Founder authorization.

---

## Characteristics

### Evidence-Based

Recommendations are grounded in repository evidence.

They emerge from constitutional analysis rather than preference or expectation.

### Advisory

A recommendation advises.
It does not authorize.

Its role is to identify the most appropriate governance artifact based upon institutional assessment.

### Explicit

Recommendations should clearly identify:

- the institutional question,
- the recommended governance artifact,
- the reasoning supporting that recommendation,
- alternative paths considered.

This transparency enables informed Founder review.

### Non-Binding

The Founder remains free to:

- accept the recommendation,
- request refinement,
- choose an alternative,
- decline further governance activity.

The recommendation informs constitutional judgment.
It does not replace it.

The recommendation connects institutional understanding with institutional decision-making.

## Common Misunderstandings

### "A recommendation authorizes work."

Incorrect.

A recommendation identifies what institutional assessment considers appropriate.
Authorization requires a separate Founder decision.

### "Recommendations predict final constitutional outcomes."

No.

A recommendation identifies the next governance artifact.
The content of that artifact remains subject to drafting, review, revision, and ratification.

### "Recommendations must always be accepted."

No.

Institutional recommendations support Founder judgment.
They do not replace it.

The authority to authorize work remains distinct from the authority to analyze institutional evidence.

## Repository References

Institutional Recommendations appear within Governance Cycle records, governance assessments, Founder decision documentation, and the Two-Gate governance model.

Together these artifacts illustrate the separation between institutional advice and institutional authorization.

---

## Reading This Concept

Institutional Recommendation should be understood as the institution's considered advice to itself.

It represents the point at which careful assessment becomes a clearly articulated proposal for future governance.

By preserving this stage separately, Project Garuda ensures that institutional analysis remains visible even when subsequent authorization or ratification differs from the original recommendation.

---

## Key Insight

Institutional Recommendation reflects one of the central lessons of the First Constitutional Epoch.

Understanding what should be considered is not the same as granting permission to begin.

By preserving these as separate institutional acts, Project Garuda ensures that analysis remains independent, authority remains explicit, and future generations can distinguish institutional reasoning from institutional authorization.

That distinction strengthens both governance and stewardship.
