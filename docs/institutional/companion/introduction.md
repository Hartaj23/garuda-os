This introduction creates no constitutional, architectural, sprint, or engineering authority.

---

Every institution develops a language.

At first, that language grows naturally.
Words emerge to describe new ideas.
Definitions become more precise as experience accumulates.

Over time, familiar terms begin carrying meanings that differ from their everyday use.

Project Garuda is no exception.

Words such as *constitution*, *authority*, *governance*, *architecture*, *stewardship*, and *Institutional HOLD* have specific meanings within the institution.

Those meanings were not invented arbitrarily.
They emerged through constitutional evolution, architectural interpretation, governance refinement, and practical experience during the First Constitutional Epoch.

This Companion exists to preserve that shared language.

It is not a constitutional artifact.
It establishes no authority.
It amends no governance.
It authorizes no engineering.

Instead, it explains the vocabulary that already exists within the institutional corpus.

Its purpose is simple.

To help every reader understand Project Garuda before attempting to interpret it.

Each entry in this Companion follows the same structure.

- **Definition** — the institutional meaning of the term.
- **Purpose** — why the concept exists.
- **Relationship** — how it connects to other institutional concepts.
- **Repository References** — where the concept appears within the canonical record.

The Companion should be read alongside Book One and the Institutional Atlas.

Book One explains how the institution evolved.
The Atlas illustrates how the institution is organized.
The Companion explains the language through which the institution understands itself.

Together, the three volumes provide narrative, structure, and vocabulary.

They do not replace the constitutional corpus.
They prepare readers to approach it with greater clarity.

Every enduring institution depends upon a shared language.

When that language is preserved carefully, future generations inherit more than definitions.
They inherit understanding.

That is the purpose of this Companion.
