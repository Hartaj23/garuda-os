---

This page has been left intentionally blank.

Not because there is nothing more to say.

Because the First Constitutional Epoch has said what it came to say.

The pages before this preserve one generation's understanding.

The pages after this belong to another.

When a future constitutional generation opens a new epoch, it should do so with its own evidence, its own questions, and its own legitimate authority.

It should never feel compelled to continue merely because space remained.

An institution does not grow because its books contain empty pages.

It grows because each generation earns the right to write upon them.

Until that day, let this page remain as it was first offered.

Blank.

Patient.

Waiting not for activity, but for legitimacy.

May every future steward who reaches this page pause before turning it.

May they remember that restraint can be as creative as action, that silence can preserve wisdom as faithfully as words, and that every enduring institution is strengthened not only by what it records, but also by what it chooses to leave unwritten.

When the next constitutional epoch truly begins, this page will no longer be empty.

It will become the bridge between two generations.

Until then, it stands as a quiet reminder of one simple truth:

Every worthy beginning first learns how to end with grace.

---

End of A Blank Page
