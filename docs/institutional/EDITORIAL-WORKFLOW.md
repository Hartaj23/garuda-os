# Editorial Workflow

This document creates no constitutional, architectural, sprint, or engineering authority.

---

## Purpose

The Editorial Workflow defines the sequence through which the First Edition of the Preservation Series shall be reviewed, refined, and prepared for archival publication.

Its purpose is consistency.

Editorial work should proceed in an orderly progression so that every refinement builds upon those completed before it.

No editorial activity shall alter constitutional authority, governance history, architectural interpretation, engineering records, or repository evidence.

The repository remains the institution's canonical source.

---

## Editorial Sequence

Editorial work shall proceed through seven phases.

```text
Phase I
Editorial Inventory
        ↓
Phase II
Structural Review
        ↓
Phase III
Language Review
        ↓
Phase IV
Reference Verification
        ↓
Phase V
Visual Review
        ↓
Phase VI
Publication Review
        ↓
Phase VII
First Edition Freeze
```

Each phase should be completed before the next begins.

---

## Phase I — Editorial Inventory

**Objective:**

Create a complete inventory of every preservation artifact.

**Confirm:**

- volumes,
- chapters,
- plates,
- entries,
- appendices,
- illustrations,
- tables,
- repository references.

The purpose of this phase is completeness rather than correction.

---

## Phase II — Structural Review

**Objective:**

Review organizational consistency across every volume.

**Verify:**

- chapter order,
- heading hierarchy,
- numbering,
- section sequencing,
- front matter,
- back matter.

The purpose is structural coherence.

---

## Phase III — Language Review

**Objective:**

Ensure a unified editorial voice.

**Review:**

- terminology,
- capitalization,
- institutional naming,
- defined concepts,
- recurring phrases,
- spelling consistency,
- punctuation consistency.

Meaning shall never change.

Only expression may be refined.

---

## Phase IV — Reference Verification

**Objective:**

Verify every institutional reference.

**Confirm:**

- repository paths,
- constitutional citations,
- governance references,
- architectural references,
- timeline references,
- cross-volume references.

Broken references shall be corrected before publication.

---

## Phase V — Visual Review

**Objective:**

Review presentation quality.

**Confirm:**

- diagrams,
- tables,
- captions,
- spacing,
- typography,
- page rhythm,
- illustration placement.

Presentation should support understanding without distracting from institutional content.

---

## Phase VI — Publication Review

**Objective:**

Prepare the Preservation Series as a unified publication.

**Verify:**

- title pages,
- copyright information (if applicable),
- edition statement,
- table of contents,
- indexes,
- bibliography,
- print formatting,
- digital formatting.

All volumes should read as components of one institutional library.

---

## Phase VII — First Edition Freeze

**Objective:**

Declare the editorial work complete.

The freeze confirms that:

- editorial review has concluded,
- presentation is stable,
- institutional references are verified,
- publication copies may be produced,
- future editorial changes require a new edition rather than silent revision.

The First Edition becomes the archival reference for the Preservation Series.

---

## Editorial Responsibilities

Editors are responsible for presentation.

They are not responsible for institutional authority.

If an editor discovers a potential constitutional, architectural, governance, or historical concern, editorial work pauses while the issue is referred to the appropriate institutional authority.

Editorial judgment never replaces institutional governance.

---

## Success Criteria

The Editorial Workflow is complete when:

- every preservation volume presents a consistent editorial standard,
- every institutional reference is accurate,
- every visual element is finalized,
- every cross-reference has been verified,
- the complete Preservation Series is suitable for long-term archival preservation.

## Closing Reflection

The First Constitutional Epoch demonstrated how an institution can create legitimate authority through disciplined governance.

The First Edition Editorial Pass demonstrates how that authority can be presented with equal discipline.

Governance preserves legitimacy.

Editorial craftsmanship preserves accessibility.

Together they ensure that future stewards inherit an institution that is not only trustworthy, but also understandable.

That is the purpose of the Editorial Workflow.
