## Epoch

**Pre-Constitutional Era**

---

## Institutional Event

Project Garuda formally adopted **Constitutional Engineering** as the discipline through which the institution would evolve.

This established the institutional commitment that constitutional legitimacy would precede architectural interpretation, and that architectural interpretation would precede engineering implementation.

From this point onward, institutional evolution would occur through explicit governance rather than informal convention.

---

## Significance

This decision transformed Project Garuda from a software initiative into a constitutional institution.

The institution no longer viewed engineering as the starting point of creation.
Instead, engineering became the final stage in a longer institutional lifecycle.

Several enduring principles emerged from this commitment:

- Constitutional authority before architecture.
- Architecture before engineering.
- Explicit authority before institutional action.
- Repository-first institutional memory.
- Stewardship across generations.

These principles became the intellectual foundation of the First Constitutional Epoch.

---

## Institutional Context

At this stage:

- constitutional artifacts had not yet been ratified,
- architectural interpretation had not yet begun,
- engineering foundations had not yet been authorized.

The institution had established its governing discipline before establishing its governing documents.

---

## Why This Entry Matters

Every discipline begins by defining its method.

For Project Garuda, Constitutional Engineering became that method.

It established that institutional legitimacy would never be inferred from technical capability.

Instead, legitimacy would emerge through explicit constitutional reasoning, disciplined governance, and transparent institutional memory.

This decision shaped every constitutional extension, architectural interpretation, governance cycle, engineering sprint, and preservation effort that followed.

## Legacy

The adoption of Constitutional Engineering established the enduring rhythm of Project Garuda.

The institution would no longer ask only:

> *"Can this be built?"*

It would first ask:

> *"Has the institution earned the authority to build it?"*

That question became one of the defining characteristics of the First Constitutional Epoch and continues to guide the institution's understanding of legitimacy, governance, and stewardship.
