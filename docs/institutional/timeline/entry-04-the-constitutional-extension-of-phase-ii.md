## Epoch

**The First Constitutional Epoch**

---

## Institutional Event

The institution ratified **GAR-0017**, establishing the constitutional extension that formally introduced the Interface Foundation as the first foundation of the descriptive external-capability epoch.

This constitutional act opened Phase II of institutional evolution and established the first constitutional boundary between Project Garuda and its external environment.

It became the constitutional starting point for the external-capability layer.

---

## Significance

GAR-0017 represented more than the authorization of a new foundation.

It demonstrated that constitutional evolution would continue through explicit institutional reasoning rather than through incremental technical expansion.

By defining Interface as a distinct constitutional concern, the institution recognized that interaction with the external world required its own constitutional legitimacy before architectural interpretation or engineering implementation could begin.

This decision established the pattern that would later guide Integration, Runtime, and the constitutional evaluation culminating in GAR-0020.

---

## Institutional Context

At the time of ratification:

- the descriptive external-capability epoch had begun,
- Interface became the first constitutionally recognized external-capability foundation,
- architectural interpretation would subsequently proceed through the approved architectural process,
- engineering remained dependent upon future authorization.

The constitutional chain expanded while preserving the principle of Authority Before Action.

---

## Why This Entry Matters

GAR-0017 introduced the constitutional language that defined the external-capability epoch.

Rather than treating external interaction as an implementation concern, the institution recognized it as a constitutional concern.

That decision fundamentally shaped the architectural and engineering evolution that followed.

## Legacy

GAR-0017 established the first constitutional step of the descriptive external-capability layer.

Its importance lies not only in introducing the Interface Foundation, but in demonstrating that constitutional legitimacy would continue to guide institutional expansion one carefully authorized foundation at a time.

It became the opening movement of a constitutional sequence that ultimately concluded with GAR-0020 and the recognition of descriptive completion.
