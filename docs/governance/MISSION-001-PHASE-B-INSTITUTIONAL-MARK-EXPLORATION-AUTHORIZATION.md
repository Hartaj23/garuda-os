## Founder Decision

Institutional Mark Exploration v1.0 is hereby authorized.

Authorization is limited to exploring what institutional mark belongs naturally on a Project Garuda page.

This is not logo design.

An institutional mark belongs to an institution.

A logo belongs to a brand.

Project Garuda has never behaved like a brand.

---

## Basis

This authorization follows the print exercise recorded in [Print Exercise — Independent Reader Observation (Mission 001 Phase B)](../institutional/PRINT-EXERCISE-INDEPENDENT-READER-OBSERVATION-MISSION-001-PHASE-B.md).

The Canonical Page Specification has demonstrated that:

- identity remains recognizably Garuda without a mark,
- identity survives monochrome reproduction,
- identity emerges from structure rather than ornament,
- the mark will acknowledge an existing identity rather than manufacture one.

The institution has earned its mark — not because it needs one, but because it has proven it no longer depends on one.

---

## Scope

Institutional Mark Exploration v1.0 may define:

- the design question the mark must answer,
- evaluation criteria for candidate marks,
- placement behavior on the Canonical Page,
- scale and proportion constraints,
- monochrome and reproduction requirements,
- relationship to typography and page structure,
- institutional archetypes for reference,
- prohibitions and boundaries for exploration.

The exploration shall describe visual and institutional behavior rather than implementation technology.

No final mark shall be selected or adopted.

No favicon shall be created.

No CSS shall be authored.

No website implementation shall occur.

No production deployment shall occur.

---

## Explicitly Not Authorized

This authorization does not permit:

- final institutional mark selection or adoption,
- favicon design,
- logo files (SVG, PNG, or equivalent),
- illustrations beyond textual exploration examples,
- CSS implementation,
- component implementation,
- website deployment,
- public announcement,
- branding collateral.

Those activities remain subject to future Founder authorization.

---

## Success Criteria

Institutional Mark Exploration v1.0 is complete when:

- the design question is reframed from brand logo to institutional acknowledgment,
- evaluation criteria are measurable and derived from the Canonical Page Specification,
- placement on the Canonical Page is defined without requiring the mark to carry identity,
- reviewers agree the mark would acknowledge an identity the page has already established,
- exploration remains consistent with Visual Philosophy, Design Language, and Design Tokens.

---

## Institutional Guidance

The page precedes the mark.

The institution precedes the brand.

The mark shall not carry the institution.

It shall simply acknowledge it.

The repository remains the authority.

When mark selection is authorized, the question will not be *"What logo should Garuda have?"* but *"What mark belongs naturally on a Garuda page?"*

---

End of Institutional Mark Exploration v1.0 Authorization
