## Founder Decision

Institutional Mark Selection v1.0 is hereby authorized.

This authorization permits the disciplined exploration and comparative evaluation of a limited number of institutional mark candidates in accordance with the approved Institutional Mark Exploration v1.0.

The purpose of this mission is not to invent an identity.

The purpose is to identify the mark that most faithfully acknowledges the identity already established by Project Garuda's visual philosophy, design language, design tokens, and Canonical Page Specification.

---

## Scope

The mission authorizes:

- creation of no more than seven institutional mark candidates,
- written rationale for every candidate against each approved evaluation criterion,
- monochrome presentation,
- application at 24px and 16px,
- application to the Canonical Page,
- application to representative print contexts,
- comparative scoring using the approved institutional evaluation framework.

---

## Explicitly Not Authorized

This authorization does not permit:

- adoption of a final institutional mark,
- favicon creation,
- CSS implementation,
- website implementation,
- production deployment,
- public announcement,
- modification of previously approved brand artifacts.

Institutional Mark Adoption remains a separate Founder decision.

---

## Success Criteria

Institutional Mark Selection v1.0 is complete when:

- every candidate has been evaluated against the established institutional criteria,
- comparative evidence has been recorded,
- one candidate clearly demonstrates the strongest institutional fit,
- sufficient evidence exists for a future Founder decision regarding adoption.

Selection recommends.

Adoption commits.

The institution shall not confuse the two.

---

## Institutional Guidance

Project Garuda's institutional mark shall be chosen with the same discipline through which the institution established its constitutional, architectural, governance, engineering, and preservation foundations.

The mark exists to acknowledge the institution.

The institution does not exist to justify the mark.
