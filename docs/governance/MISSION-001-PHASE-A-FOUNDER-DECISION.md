## Authority Constraint

This document records a Founder **decision** and **authorization** for Phase A only.

It creates no constitutional, architectural, sprint, or engineering authority.

It does not authorize public deployment, brand finalization, or external communications.

---

## Decision

Mission 001 is hereby authorized to begin.

Authorization is limited to **Phase A — Site Architecture only**.

No authorization is granted at this stage for:

- Brand Foundation (Phase B)
- Content Integration (Phase C)
- Research & News (Phase D)
- Public deployment
- Custom domain
- Social channels
- External communications

Those activities remain subject to separate Founder authorization following successful review of Phase A.

---

## Scope

Phase A shall establish the structural foundation of Project Garuda's future public website.

The implementation shall include:

- repository-based website structure at `website/`,
- information architecture,
- navigation hierarchy,
- public reading journey,
- internal linking strategy,
- markdown organization,
- implementation documentation.

No visual design decisions shall be considered final during this phase.

No production deployment shall occur.

No public announcement shall be made.

---

## Success Criteria

Phase A is complete when:

- the information architecture is coherent,
- navigation reflects institutional priorities,
- repository references are correct,
- the public reading journey is complete,
- the implementation remains repository-first,
- the structure is suitable for subsequent review.

Completion of Phase A authorizes no further work.

Subsequent phases require explicit Founder approval.

---

## Institutional Guidance

Project Garuda shall communicate with the same discipline through which it was created.

The public website is not intended to simplify the institution.

It is intended to make the institution approachable without reducing its intellectual integrity.

Repository authority remains unchanged.

The website serves as an introduction.

The repository remains the institution.

---
