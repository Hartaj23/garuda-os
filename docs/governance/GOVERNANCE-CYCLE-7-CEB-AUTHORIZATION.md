## Evidence Integrity

The Constitutional Evidence Base shall remain **descriptive** throughout its development.

Researchers may record competing schools of thought, contradictory evidence, unresolved questions,
and minority viewpoints without resolving them.

Resolution occurs only during constitutional synthesis.

Evidence shall not be rewritten to support predetermined constitutional conclusions.

This clause protects the CEB from confirmation bias. The corpus must remain a faithful record of
what is discovered, including where experts disagree.

Evidence Integrity operates together with the Permanent Governance Doctrine — Principle of
Constitutional Humility — recorded in the Cycle 7 Founder Decision.

---

## CEB Drafting Scope

Authorized CEB work shall:

1. Establish the CEB as the authoritative research corpus for Book Two
2. Remain descriptive — never normative constitutional law
3. Preserve technology neutrality
4. Seek enduring principles rather than popular investors or fashionable methods
5. Document consensus, disagreement, historical evidence, academic research, practitioner
   experience, and institutional knowledge when properly cited
6. Evaluate candidate observations against universality, timelessness, evidence, technology
   neutrality, and constitutional necessity — without elevating them to constitutional articles
7. Defer all normative resolution to a future, separately authorized constitutional synthesis
   (GAR-0021 path — not authorized here)

CEB drafting shall **not** authorize architecture, sprint work, or engineering.
