This charter governs how constitutional evidence is produced within the Constitutional Evidence Base.
It is **not** a GAR constitution. It creates no GAR-0021, architectural, sprint, or engineering
authority.

---

## Preamble

Before an institution may define how intelligence shall reason about investment, it must first
establish how evidence itself shall be gathered, preserved, challenged, and elevated.

CEB-0000 is that establishment.

It is the Constitution of Evidence for Book Two — not law over systems, but discipline over inquiry.

Most projects begin with code and add documentation later.
Project Garuda constructs intellectual scaffolding first, so that every future implementation may
trace its lineage to evidence and, only thereafter, to constitutional principle.

This charter answers one question:

> How must constitutional evidence itself be produced?

---

## Article I — Purpose

**§1.** The Constitutional Evidence Base exists to discover enduring principles governing investment
intelligence through disciplined inquiry.

**§2.** The CEB records, organizes, and synthesizes evidence regarding markets, behaviour, economics,
businesses, market structure, trading, risk, decision-making, regulation, and comparative schools of
investing.

**§3.** The CEB exists so that every constitutional principle later adopted within Investment
Intelligence can be traced to documented evidence rather than opinion, fashion, or convenience.

**§4.** The CEB does not exist to select winners among investors, vendors, technologies, or products.

---

## Article II — Authority

**§1.** The CEB is **descriptive**.

**§2.** The CEB establishes no constitutional obligations.

**§3.** The CEB authorizes no architectural work.

**§4.** The CEB authorizes no engineering, sprint, production implementation, AI model selection,
trading logic, API design, broker integration, or product feature work.

**§5.** Constitutional authority begins only after principles are synthesized, reviewed, and ratified
through a separately authorized constitution — presently contemplated as **GAR-0021**, which is
**not** authorized by this charter.

**§6.** This charter governs the CEB corpus only. It does not amend GAR-0001 through GAR-0020.

**§7.** When this charter conflicts with a Founder Decision or CEB Authorization record, the higher
governance artifact prevails for questions of institutional permission. When it conflicts with a
ratified GAR constitution, the constitution prevails.

## Article IV — Research Method

**§1. Constitutional Inquiry**

CEB research asks what must be true across time, geography, technology, and asset class where a claim
applies — not merely what economists, traders, or popular authors currently say.

**§2. Collection**

Evidence may be drawn from historical record, academic research, practitioner experience,
institutional knowledge, market structure, regulatory frameworks, and comparative philosophy —
provided sources are documented.

**§3. Disagreement**

Disagreement is not failure. Competing schools, contradictory findings, unresolved questions, and
minority viewpoints shall be recorded without forced resolution during evidence development.

**§4. Conflicting Schools**

Comparative treatment shall prefer faithful representation over premature synthesis. Schools may be
contrasted; they shall not be erased to manufacture consensus.

**§5. Source Evaluation**

Sources shall be evaluated for relevance, credibility, scope, datedness, and bias. Evaluation does
not require rejection of imperfect sources; it requires honest characterization of their limits.

**§6. Observation vs Conclusion**

| Kind | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Observation** | A documented finding or claim as it appears in evidence |
| **Evidence Record** | A curated, citable statement of observation within the CEB |
| **Comparative Review** | Examination of agreement and conflict across records |
| **Candidate Principle** | An observation that has survived elevation criteria — still not law |
| **Constitutional Principle** | A principle ratified through constitutional governance — outside CEB authority |

Observations shall not be silently rewritten as conclusions. Conclusions that claim constitutional
force are prohibited within the CEB.

**§7. Evidence Integrity**

The CEB shall remain descriptive throughout its development.

Resolution of competing evidence occurs only during constitutional synthesis.

Evidence shall not be rewritten to support predetermined constitutional conclusions.

---

## Article V — Elevation Criteria

Every observation considered for eventual constitutional elevation shall be evaluated against five
permanent tests:

| Criterion | Question |
| --- | --- |
| **Universality** | Does it hold across markets, geographies, and asset classes where the claim applies? |
| **Timelessness** | Does it endure beyond a particular technology, decade, or fashion? |
| **Evidence** | Is it supported by documented inquiry rather than assertion? |
| **Technology neutrality** | Can it be stated without committing to a platform, vendor, or implementation? |
| **Constitutional necessity** | Would future architecture or implementation be incomplete or unsafe without it? |

**§1.** Survival of these criteria confers **candidacy** only.

**§2.** Candidacy is not ratification.

**§3.** Elevation to constitutional law requires separately authorized constitutional process.

**§4.** These five tests are permanent to the CEB unless this charter is amended under Founder
authorization.

---

## Article VI — Citation Principles

**§1.** Every constitutional candidate principle shall be traceable to one or more evidence records.

**§2.** Traceability is a defining characteristic of Book Two. A principle that cannot be audited back
to evidence is not ready for constitutional consideration.

**§3.** Citations shall identify sources with sufficient precision that a future steward can locate
and re-examine them.

**§4.** Absence of citation is not stylistic preference; it is a defect in constitutional readiness.

**§5.** The CEB may cite primary sources, secondary analyses, and institutional records. Character of
the source shall be clear.

---

## Article VII — Evidence Lifecycle

The path from discovery to constitutional law shall remain transparent:

```text
Observation
    ↓
Evidence Record
    ↓
Comparative Review
    ↓
Cross-domain Analysis
    ↓
Candidate Principle
    ↓
Constitutional Review
    ↓
GAR-0021
```

**§1.** Each stage is distinct. Advancement is not automatic.

**§2.** Cross-domain analysis examines whether a candidate survives contact with other CEB volumes.

**§3.** Constitutional Review and GAR-0021 lie beyond CEB authority. This lifecycle describes the
intended bridge; it does not authorize crossing it.

**§4.** Volume XII — Constitutional Synthesis — may organize candidate principles for Founder and
Architect review. Synthesis within the CEB remains descriptive until a constitution is separately
authorized and ratified.

---

## Article VIII — Principle of Reversibility

**§1.** Evidence may be corrected.

**§2.** Evidence may be expanded.

**§3.** Evidence may be superseded by stronger evidence.

**§4.** Constitutional authority changes only through constitutional governance.

This principle separates the evolving knowledge base from the stable constitutional layer.

An intelligence expected to learn continuously may revise its evidence.
It may not silently revise the law that governs it.

---

## Article IX — Scope and Exclusions

**§1. Within scope**

Markets; human behaviour; economics and macroeconomics; businesses and value creation; market
structure; trading and probability; risk and portfolio intelligence; decision intelligence;
regulation and market integrity; the Indian investment ecosystem; comparative schools of investing;
and constitutional synthesis of evidence.

**§2. Explicitly excluded**

Implementation-specific technologies; software designs; programming languages; platform decisions;
broker integrations; APIs; trading logic; product features; AI model selection; sprint planning; and
engineering work.

## Article XI — Roles

**§1.** The **Chief Systems Architect** authors and maintains the CEB under Founder authorization.

constitution, architecture, and engineering.

**§3.** The **Principal Implementation Engineer** resumes responsibility only after an ADR and sprint

## Article XII — Mottos

**Constitutional Engineering Motto**

> Discover before defining. Define before designing. Design before building.

**Investment Intelligence Motto**

> Reason before Recommendation.

The second motto is recorded here as Book Two aspiration. It creates no product, model, or
engineering obligation within the CEB.

---

## Article XIII — Relation to Permanent Governance Doctrine

The Principle of Constitutional Humility, recorded in the Governance Cycle 7 Founder Decision,
applies to all elevation from this corpus:

> No constitutional principle shall be adopted merely because it appears persuasive. Every
> constitutional principle shall survive disciplined inquiry, comparative analysis, and documented
> evidence before it may govern future architecture or implementation.

CEB-0000 operationalizes that doctrine for evidence production. It does not replace it.

---

## Article XIV — Amendments

**§1.** This charter may be amended only under explicit Founder authorization.

**§2.** Amendment of CEB-0000 does not amend any GAR constitution.

**§3.** Correction of evidence under Article VIII does not require amendment of this charter unless
the method of evidence production itself changes.

---

## Article XV — Interpretation

When ambiguity exists within the CEB, interpretation shall favor:

1. Descriptive fidelity over persuasive narrative
2. Traceability over elegance
3. Disagreement preserved over false consensus
4. Technology neutrality over implementation convenience
5. Constitutional Humility over premature elevation
6. Reversibility of evidence over silent revision of law
7. Repository authority over conversational inference
