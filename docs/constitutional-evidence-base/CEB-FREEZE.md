## 5. Relationship to CEB-0000 Reversibility

CEB-0000 Article VIII (Principle of Reversibility) provides that evidence may be corrected, expanded, or superseded — while constitutional authority changes only through constitutional governance.

Under this freeze:

- **Edition 1.0** is the fixed reference for any immediately subsequent constitutional drafting gate
- Corrections that would alter Edition 1.0 meaning require a governed amendment or a new edition
- Expansion into new domains or volumes is **Part II+ / Edition 2.0+** work — not silent accretion into 1.0 during drafting
- Supersession of 1.0, if ever warranted, shall be explicit and versioned

Reversibility protects truth-seeking across time.
Freeze protects the integrity of the source while authority is derived from it.
