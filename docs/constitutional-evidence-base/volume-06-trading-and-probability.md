## Inquiry 1 — What Is a Decision Under Uncertainty?

### The Question

What must be true of an act before it counts as a decision in a probabilistic environment?

### Investigation

A decision under uncertainty is a commitment of scarce resources (capital, attention, reputation, time) among alternatives whose consequences are not fully known in advance. It is not a wish, a forecast alone, or a narrative. It is an act that closes some options and opens path-dependent futures.

Volume I established uncertainty beyond neat risk. Volume II established capacity limits and emotion. Volume III established survivability prior to optimization. Volume VI asks what follows when the agent must **choose anyway**.

Deterministic environments allow correct/incorrect in a strong sense. Probabilistic environments allow better/worse processes whose individual outcomes can still disappoint. That shift is constitutional for any intelligence that advises action.

### Established Observations

- Action under incomplete knowledge is the normal condition of trading and investing.
- A forecast without a commitment is not yet a decision.
- Outcomes alone do not fully grade decision quality when luck is present.

### Competing Perspectives

- Decisions should wait for near-certainty vs. waiting is itself a decision with opportunity cost.
- Formal expected-utility frameworks are adequate vs. too narrow for Knightian uncertainty and path dependence.
- "Do nothing" is neutral vs. often an active position (cash, benchmark, career risk).

### Candidate Constitutional Insights

- Investment intelligence must treat recommendation as decision support under uncertainty — not as announcement of determined truth.
- Outcome ≠ decision quality when environments are probabilistic.

## Inquiry 2 — What Is Probability — as Used by Decision-Makers?

### The Question

What kinds of probability claims are admissible — and which silently overclaim?

### Investigation

Probability appears as:

- **Frequentist** — long-run rates in repeatable trials
- **Subjective / Bayesian** — degrees of belief updated with evidence
- **Model-implied** — outputs of calibrated engines (options, risk models)
- **Narrative probability** — "likely" as social language without numbers

Markets force agents to act as if probabilities exist even when the generating process is contested (Volume I). The constitutional hazard is **false precision**: reporting 67.3% when the honest statement is "uncertain, leaning positive, model-dependent."

Volume II overconfidence and Volume V signal/noise meet here: numbers can launder narrative into apparent science.

### Established Observations

- Agents use multiple incompatible senses of "probability" without noticing.
- Model-implied probabilities inherit model error and regime risk (Volume III adaptation).
- Verbal likelihoods are poorly calibrated across people and contexts.

### Competing Perspectives

- All uncertainty reduces to subjective probability vs. some uncertainty forbids meaningful numerical assignment.
- Market prices are the best probability aggregator vs. systematically biased aggregators.
- More decimal places improve decisions vs. often fake precision.

### Candidate Constitutional Insights

- Probability claims must declare their type and limits — or they mislead.
- Investment intelligence should prefer calibrated humility over spurious numeric certainty.

## Inquiry 3 — What Is the Distinction Between Confidence and Certainty?

### The Question

When may an agent be confident — and when is "certainty" a category error?

### Investigation

A constitutional distinction (watchlist):

| Concept | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Confidence** | A graded stance about a belief or decision process, ideally calibrated to evidence and track record |
| **Certainty** | The claim that doubt has been eliminated — rarely justified in open market systems |

Confidence can be high when evidence is strong and mechanisms are understood. Certainty about future prices, regimes, or others' behaviour conflicts with Volumes I–V. Trading cultures that demand certainty produce overbetting; advisory cultures that perform certainty produce false trust.

### Established Observations

- Markets repeatedly punish certainty narratives.
- Calibrated confidence is rarer than expressed confidence (Volume II).
- Refusing certainty is compatible with decisive action under process.

### Competing Perspectives

- Strong conviction is required for outsized returns vs. strong conviction is a common path to ruin.
- Certainty language is harmless rhetoric vs. causally increases sizing errors.
- Experts should express narrow intervals vs. experts should express wider intervals than novices expect.

### Candidate Constitutional Insights

- Confidence vs certainty is a distinction investment intelligence must enforce in language and sizing logic.
- An intelligence that speaks with certainty about open futures violates the environment it inhabits.

## Inquiry 4 — What Is Edge?

### The Question

What must be true for a probabilistic advantage to exist — and what destroys it?

### Investigation

Edge is a positive expected advantage after costs, relative to a relevant alternative (cash, benchmark, opposing flow), arising from information, analysis, structure, behaviour of others, or preferential access — and **decaying under adaptation** (Volume III).

Without edge, trading is a costly transfer plus risk. With illusory edge, agents pay for randomness dressed as skill. Distinguishing edge from luck requires method (Volume II learning) and enough trials — which many agents never obtain before ruin (survivability).

Structure (Volume V) and costs determine whether a theoretical edge is executable.

### Established Observations

- Gross forecast skill can be erased by costs, impact, and fees.
- Edges attract capital and imitation; crowding reduces them.
- Small-sample success is weak evidence of edge.

### Competing Perspectives

- Persistent edge is nearly impossible in liquid markets vs. persistent in niches, skill, or structural privilege.
- Edge should be statistically proven before action vs. some edges are structural and visible without large samples.
- Retail cannot have edge vs. can have behavioural or horizon-based edge against institutions.

### Candidate Constitutional Insights

- Action that assumes edge without a falsifiable source and cost accounting is constitutionally incomplete.
- Edge is perishable under adaptation — not a permanent entitlement.

## Inquiry 5 — What Is Decision Quality Distinct From Outcome Quality?

### The Question

How should processes be judged when good decisions can lose and bad decisions can win?

### Investigation

In probabilistic domains, **decision quality** concerns the process: information used, alternatives considered, probabilities and payoffs reasoned, size relative to ruin, and precommitments under emotion. **Outcome quality** is what happened.

Poker, medicine, military strategy, and trading share this separation. Volume II noisy feedback makes outcome-worship especially dangerous: it trains agents to repeat luck and abandon skill.

This is related to signal/noise (Volume V) applied to evaluation of one's own acts.

### Established Observations

- Short-horizon outcomes are dominated by variance for many strategies.
- Process review improves calibration more than outcome celebration/shame alone.
- Organizations that reward only outcomes encourage hidden risk and narrative luck.

### Competing Perspectives

- Only wealth matters ex post — process talk is cope vs. process is the only controllable object.
- Track records sufficiently reveal decision quality vs. track records without context conceal risk taken.
- Checklists help experts vs. constrain experts.

### Candidate Constitutional Insights

- Decision quality and outcome quality must not be conflated.
- Investment intelligence should evaluate and explain process — not only predicted or realized P&L.

## Inquiry 6 — How Does Feedback Govern Learning in Trading Environments?

### The Question

What enduring properties of feedback make probabilistic domains hard to learn?

### Investigation

Feedback may be delayed, sparse, confounded, asymmetric (losses felt more — Volume II), or structurally biased (survivorship). Trading shortens some feedback loops and shortens lives via ruin. Investing lengthens loops until learning barely occurs within a career.

Volume II established that experience ≠ wisdom without method. Volume VI adds: **the cadence and cleanliness of feedback set the ceiling on learning speed**. Systems that update beliefs tick-by-tick overfit noise (Volume V). Systems that never update ossify.

### Established Observations

- Noisy, delayed feedback impairs calibration.
- Overfitting to recent feedback is a recurring failure mode.
- Structured post-mortems and base rates improve learning relative to narrative memory.

### Competing Perspectives

- Faster feedback always helps vs. faster feedback increases noise-fitting and addiction-like behaviour.
- Discretionary learning beats systematic rules vs. rules beat biased memory.
- Markets are too non-stationary to learn from vs. some invariants (costs, behaviour, structure) remain learnable.

### Candidate Constitutional Insights

- Feedback design is part of decision intelligence — not an afterthought.
- Updating frequency must match signal timescale or it becomes noise harvesting.

## Inquiry 7 — What Is Risk Versus Volatility in Action?

### The Question

When agents trade, which "risk" are they managing — and which word do they misuse?

### Investigation

Constitutional distinction (watchlist; foreshadowed in Volumes I and III):

| Concept | Meaning in action |
| --- | --- |
| **Volatility** | Variability of price or returns over a horizon — often measurable |
| **Risk** | Possibility of failing to meet an objective: ruin, permanent loss, shortfall, mandate breach, existential failure |

Traders may need volatility for sizing and options. Owners may accept volatility while rejecting ruin. Conflating them produces both fear of healthy variance and blindness to silent lethal risks (liquidity, solvency, concentration, path).

### Established Observations

- Agents routinely say "risk" when they mean "volatility," and the reverse.
- Low volatility can coexist with high ruin risk (leverage, gap, liquidity).
- High volatility can coexist with controlled ruin risk (small size, defined exit, non-recourse limits).

### Competing Perspectives

- Variance-based risk measures are sufficient for practice vs. dangerously incomplete.
- Drawdown limits operationalize risk adequately vs. still miss gap and correlation breaks.
- Subjective risk (sleep at night) is legitimate input vs. merely emotion to suppress.

### Candidate Constitutional Insights

- Risk vs volatility must remain distinct in any probabilistic decision framework.
- Sizing and advice must state which failure modes are being controlled.

## Inquiry 8 — What Enduring Role Does Position Size Play?

### The Question

Why is size not a detail — but a constitutional component of probabilistic decision?

### Investigation

In multiplicative wealth dynamics, size determines whether a series of bets compounds or extinguishes. The same "edge" with reckless size becomes ruin; with tiny size becomes irrelevance. Kelly-type reasoning, practitioner lore, and ruin theory converge: **size encodes respect for uncertainty**.

Volume II emotion and Volume III survivability appear as sizing failures: euphoria scales up; fear scales down at the worst times; career incentives distort size.

### Established Observations

- Size transforms the distribution of outcomes more than many agents admit.
- Leverage is size by another name with harder constraints.
- Path dependence makes sequence of wins/losses matter given size.

### Competing Perspectives

- Full Kelly is optimal vs. fractional Kelly or discretionary underestimation of edge is mandatory.
- Fixed fractional rules beat discretionary sizing vs. context-sensitive sizing adds value.
- "Concentrate when sure" vs. concentration is how certainty language kills.

### Candidate Constitutional Insights

- Position size is part of the decision — not a downstream implementation detail.
- Intelligence that recommends direction without size/risk framing recommends an incomplete act.

## Inquiry 9 — What Is Expectancy — and What Does a Distribution Hide?

### The Question

When is "positive expectancy" enough — and when is the shape of outcomes decisive?

### Investigation

Expectancy (mean outcome per unit risk or per trade) is a useful compression. Distributions with identical means can differ in skew, kurtosis, and path to ruin. Selling crash insurance can show high expectancy until it does not. Trend systems can show low hit rates with positive expectancy.

Volume I fat tails and Volume III non-ergodicity warn: **the mean across parallel worlds is not the life of one agent**. Trading under probability requires distributional honesty, not only a green expected value.

### Established Observations

- Hit rate and expectancy are different objects.
- Left-tail strategies can look excellent until absorbing failure.
- Sample means converge slowly — or not at all — under heavy tails.

### Competing Perspectives

- Optimize expected utility / growth vs. optimize drawdown-constrained criteria.
- Truncate left tails at all costs vs. some left-tail exposure is the price of return.
- Monte Carlo path analysis is essential vs. false comfort from misspecified generators.

### Candidate Constitutional Insights

- Expectancy without distribution and ruin context is an incomplete probabilistic claim.
- Investment intelligence should expose path and tail — not only central tendency.

## Inquiry 10 — What Is Process Versus Prediction?

### The Question

Is the enduring skill forecasting events — or governing behaviour when forecasts are wrong?

### Investigation

Prediction seeks to name future states. Process seeks to specify what will be done across states: entries, exits, size, refusal, review. In domains with low forecast base rates, process dominates. In domains with stable edges, prediction and process intertwine.

Volume II discipline as precommitment and Volume V discontinuity imply: **the agent will face surprise**. Systems that only predict without process fail at the moment of surprise. Systems that only process without any edge may still lose slowly to costs.

### Established Observations

- Forecast accuracy is often modest at horizons people care about.
- Precommitted process reduces emotion-driven error under surprise.
- Refusal conditions ("no trade") are part of process competence.

### Competing Perspectives

- Superior prediction is the essence of trading skill vs. superior risk process is the essence.
- Discretion around process is necessary expertise vs. discretion is where process dies.
- AI should maximize predictive score vs. maximize decision-process adherence under uncertainty.

### Candidate Constitutional Insights

- Process under error is as constitutive of trading intelligence as prediction before error.
- An intelligence that predicts without refusal and sizing process is unfinished.

## Inquiry 12 — What Must Investment Intelligence Assume Before Advising Action?

### The Question

Before recommending a probabilistic act, what defaults should an honest system treat as mandatory?

### Investigation

Descriptive defaults (not law):

1. The environment is probabilistic; certainty language is presumptively false
2. Probability claims need type, horizon, and humility
3. Confidence must be separable from certainty
4. Edge claims need source, costs, and decay awareness
5. Decision quality ≠ outcome quality
6. Feedback is noisy; update cadence can harm or help
7. Risk ≠ volatility; failure modes must be named
8. Size is part of the decision
9. Expectancy needs distribution and ruin context
10. Process and refusal matter as much as prediction
11. Prior volume constraints still bind (structure, behaviour, credit, enterprise)
12. "Buy/sell" without uncertainty, size, and failure framing is an incomplete speech act

### Established Observations

- Advice cultures that omit these defaults produce characteristic harms: overbetting, blame-by-outcome, and false certainty.
- Users experience recommendations as decisions even when systems label them "information."

### Competing Perspectives

- How much probabilistic machinery to expose to end users vs. keep internal.
- Whether AI must refuse when edge cannot be articulated (Volume VIII deepens refusal).
- Universal defaults vs. different defaults for owners vs speculators (Volume V distinction).

### Candidate Constitutional Insights

- Investment intelligence advising action inherits the full constraint stack of probabilistic decision-making.
- Completeness of a recommendation is measured by uncertainty, edge, size, process, and failure modes — not by directional bravado.
